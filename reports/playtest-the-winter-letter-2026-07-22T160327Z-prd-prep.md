### PRD Candidate: FACT Audience-Visibility Reveal-Concealment Alignment

Candidate role: first
Purpose: ensure that a FACT whose `audience_visibility` promises reader-concealment either produces a compiled prose-prompt concealment instruction or is truthfully re-scoped so authors are told the field does not conceal from the reader and are routed to the SECRET reveal-concealment model — so an author cannot gain false confidence that a hidden hard-canon premise is protected by a field whose value the prose prompt ignores.
Sources: F024 (constrained by strengths F010, F012, F013)
Problem: The compiled prose prompt's `<audience_knowledge>` block ("Audience already knows", "Audience does not know", "Dramatic irony allowed now", and the ambiguous-perception line) is populated exclusively from active SECRET records keyed on the SECRET's `audience_visibility` and `pov_access`. Meanwhile the FACT `audience_visibility` field guidance tells the author `hidden` means "Do not expose this fact to the audience yet." This can quietly leak a premise, and it means the prior lineage's "reveal-lock decision" (aligning the two entangled facts' visibility) has no observable prose-prompt effect.
Product rule or seam: Audience-level reader concealment of a truth the POV knows is modeled through the SECRET record (`pov_access`, `audience_visibility`, `reveal_permission`), whose `hidden` audience_visibility already populates "Audience does not know" deterministically.
