## Reassessment Verdict

The reported product gap is real and narrower than the earlier broad
**Causally Due Developments** candidate. Continuity Loom's prose prompt already
renders complete active/onstage dossiers and tells the writer to express
non-POV psychology through visible behavior, speech, timing, silence, posture,
gesture, object handling, and subtext. Existing Ideate also receives full
active dossiers. Neither surface gives the author a deterministic,
character-indexed brainstorming contract before prose generation.

Ideate cannot close this gap through its existing Author Focus field. Its
operator slots are assigned from pressure records; Author Focus is explicitly
forbidden from changing slot eligibility, assignment, grounding bundles, or
slate shrinkage; and “who acts” is only a secondary diversity preference. A
request such as “help each non-POV character act characteristically” can
therefore remain grounded yet return no dedicated alternatives for a particular
character.

The recommended first and only current PRD is **Cast Possibilities — Grounded
Non-POV Behavior for the Next Local Moment**. It creates a dedicated
pre-generation assistance surface that deterministically renders the saved
moment, complete selected story authority, and the same complete active/full
cast dossier bodies used for prose generation. It asks for exactly three
distinct, observable, premise-level behavior possibilities for every eligible
non-POV active/full character.

The output is inspectable, citation-grounded, session-only scratch. It cannot
enter the Generation Brief, a record, the active working set, a candidate, or a
prose prompt automatically. The author may keep or copy useful cards, but must
independently rewrite any chosen behavior into a canonical field and explicitly
save it.

This is a new `prose-aligned` assistance workflow under the existing
`docs/principles/FOUNDATIONS.md` §9.1 profile family. It does not require a
constitutional amendment, a new domain glossary term, or an ADR. It does
require one new prompt-template domain authority plus synchronized compiler,
schema-mapping, active-doc, user-guide, version, assurance, and browser
surfaces in the future implementation.

Supporting skill result: domain model unchanged — **Cast Possibilities** is a
workflow identity, and its cards are workflow-owned assistance output rather
than canonical story-domain concepts. No `CONTEXT.md` entry or ADR is owed.

External research: skipped. The design resolves from the repository's
constitution, active specifications, current implementation, live tracker, and
durable product evidence. No external methodological claim is needed.

## Recommended First PRD

### Cast Possibilities — Grounded Non-POV Behavior for the Next Local Moment

**Purpose:** Restore the author's pleasure and control when the next moment is
known primarily through the POV character by offering useful,
character-specific ways for every other materially active character to exert
agency.

**Problem:** The author often knows what the POV character must do but wants
other characters to respond from their dossiers rather than from prescribed
beats. Writing “Make X act characteristically” delegates that discovery to the
prose writer, which often gives X only a phrase or two. Existing Ideate can
brainstorm grounded developments but cannot guarantee three distinct,
character-indexed options for each non-POV active character.

**Product rule:** Cast Possibilities is a dedicated pre-generation,
`prose-aligned` assistance surface. It compiles the saved current moment and
complete selected authority, identifies every non-POV active/full cast member,
and returns exactly three distinct, observable, citation-grounded behavior
possibilities for each. It produces no scene prose, no dialogue lines, no
future sequence, and no canonical write.

### Entry Point And Availability

- Add **Cast Possibilities** to primary navigation.
- Add a Generation Brief link labeled in author language, such as **Explore how
  the other characters might act**.
- The surface is available only when the saved draft has:
  - a concrete, resolvable selected POV;
  - at least one different cast member in `active_onstage_cast_full`;
  - valid linked-ENTITY references and required core dossier authority for
    every eligible character; and
  - the universal current-state fields needed to identify one local moment:
    current time, current location, onstage entities, and immediate situation.
- Full prose readiness is not required. `must_render`, handoff, stop guidance,
  optional current-state detail, voice pressure, and overrides render when
  supplied, but their incompleteness does not block unless the declared source
  is structurally unsafe or the moment cannot be identified.
- If the Generation Brief view has unsaved edits, the Cast Possibilities link
  or destination explains that the current prompt uses the last saved draft
  and offers the existing Save action. Unsaved browser state is never sent or
  fingerprinted as hidden source.
- No eligible cast produces an explanatory empty state, not an OpenRouter
  action.

### Declared Source Profile

The compiler reads only:

1. Cast-Possibilities template constants and version identities.
2. The story configuration needed for story contract, content boundaries, and
   applicable POV/prose-mode restrictions.
3. The most recently saved Generation Brief draft, projected to:
   - active working set and selected POV;
   - current authoritative state;
   - immediate handoff;
   - supplied manual directive fields;
   - current cast voice pressure;
   - cast voice overrides; and
   - supplied local stop guidance.
4. Every selected active-working-set story record, rendered completely at the
   applicable authoritative prose-aligned detail site without semantic
   filtering, retrieval, ranking, or token-budget eviction.
5. User-selected cast inclusion bands for disclosure and eligibility.
6. Every eligible non-POV active/full CAST MEMBER dossier, rendered with the
   same complete dossier body bytes, field order, linked-ENTITY name, current
   voice pressure, and temporary override treatment used by prose generation.
7. Deterministic character, dossier-field, brief-field, and selected-record
   citation keys; request identity; disclosure counts; versions; prompt length
   or token estimate; and prompt fingerprint.
8. For per-character regeneration only, the explicit target character and
   that character's current normalized card summaries as an avoid-list.

The complete dossier body remains the prose authority's rendering rather than a
second summary. A separate deterministic citation legend maps dossier keys to
the character and dossier field labels without restating or rewriting their
values.

The profile excludes accepted prose, accepted-segment metadata, candidates,
rejected or superseded generations, Private Notes, prompt archives, prior
assistance output except the explicit same-surface regeneration avoid-list,
provider memory, hidden UI state, wall-clock selection, and every record outside
the user-selected active working set.

The compiler never selects a subset of eligible characters or selected records
by salience, keyword, embedding, model judgment, context budget, or hidden
state. Oversize or unrepresentable complete source fails visibly before
transport.

### Prompt And Quality Contract

The inspected prompt:

- identifies the model as a character-behavior brainstorming assistant, not a
  prose writer or plot planner;
- names the active scope and every eligible character;
- preserves the same authority order as prose generation;
- requires observable non-POV behavior unless the configured prose mode permits
  broader interiority;
- requires each possibility to arise from the character's dossier and current
  canonical pressure rather than generic scene logic;
- requires each possibility to be feasible in the current physical,
  epistemic, relational, and reveal state;
- asks for tactics, actions, interventions, assistance, refusals, withholding,
  meaningful silence, object handling, embodied presence, or speech functions,
  but never drafted dialogue or scene prose;
- prefers diversity of behavioral channel when the current affordances allow
  it and forbids three superficial dialogue variants;
- requires each possibility to change or sharpen an immediate pressure, tactic,
  access condition, relationship expression, or feasible-action set;
- forbids future sequences, branch packages, beat sheets, outlines, global
  consequences, new named entities, unsupported facts, and automatic record
  changes; and
- states that cards are independent possibilities, not guaranteed-compatible
  ensemble combinations.

### Output Contract

The model returns one shallow strict envelope:

```text
contract
characters[]
  character_key
  cards[3]
    observable_move
    character_fit
    moment_fit
    local_effect
    dossier_keys[]
    context_keys[]
    distinction
```

Rules:

- `characters` contains exactly one entry for every disclosed eligible
  character, in deterministic character order.
- `character_key` resolves to that character and appears exactly once.
- Every card field is nonblank.
- `observable_move` is premise-level behavior, not dialogue, narration, or
  scene text.
- `character_fit` explains why this character would plausibly choose the move.
- `moment_fit` explains why the move is possible and relevant now.
- `local_effect` states the immediate pressure or action-space effect without
  planning downstream story.
- `dossier_keys` contains at least one citation owned by that exact character.
- `context_keys` contains at least one resolvable saved-brief or selected-record
  citation.
- `distinction` explains the material difference from the character's other
  two cards.
- The model does not echo trusted source metadata. The server attaches project,
  character, version, source-disclosure, and fingerprint metadata only after
  local parsing.

Malformed structure, an unknown or duplicate character, a missing eligible
character, any count other than three, an unknown citation, or a dossier
citation owned by another character quarantines the complete response.
Quarantine shows a precise safe reason and raw scratch where permitted; it
never repairs, retries, or writes.

Semantic quality is prompt-led and field-evaluated. Keyword heuristics must not
claim to prove character specificity, behavioral distinctness, dialogue
absence, or plot-plan absence where ordinary language makes such inference
unsafe.

### Inspection, Send, And Freshness

- Compilation and Prompt Inspector are localhost-only.
- Source disclosure names the selected POV, eligible characters, selected
  record count and type counts, SECRET inclusion, saved-draft identity,
  prompt length or token estimate, versions, and fingerprint.
- Every request edit or relevant source save invalidates send eligibility.
- **Analyze** is the sole action that may make one OpenRouter request.
- Analyze carries the inspected fingerprint, and the server rebuilds the
  complete saved source and prompt before reading credentials or sending.
- Missing, malformed, stale, unrepresentable, oversize, capability-unknown, or
  model-incompatible input makes zero provider calls and shows the established
  targeted recovery.
- No client-supplied prompt is accepted.
- No automatic retry, output repair, fallback, model switch, or provider fork
  exists.

### Result Interaction And Persistence

- Group cards beneath each character's linked-ENTITY name.
- Cards may be kept, copied, or cleared.
- Copying selected cards creates plain clipboard text only. It does not label
  the text canonical and does not write a Generation Brief field.
- Per-character Regenerate creates a new locally inspected request for that
  character using only that character's current card summaries as the explicit
  avoid-list. One explicit send replaces only that character's set.
- No Apply, Insert, Prefill, Accept, Create record, Update record, Change band,
  Add to working set, Use as prose, or Use in prompt action exists.
- The author independently rewrites any useful behavior into the canonical
  Generation Brief editor and explicitly saves it.
- Current cards and keepers use browser `sessionStorage`, keyed by project and
  prompt fingerprint. They may survive reload in the same browser session.
- Source changes make old cards visibly stale. Stale cards remain readable and
  copyable but cannot parameterize regeneration or any other assistance call.
- Clear removes the slate and keepers. Cleared or expired scratch leaves no
  project-store, durable-browser, backup, export, prompt, provenance, or log
  residue.

### Acceptance

- Identical declared source, request, and versions produce identical prompt
  bytes and fingerprints.
- A golden complete-source fixture proves every selected record, every eligible
  character, every complete dossier field, applicable current pressure and
  override, and every supplied purpose-facing brief field is visible without
  hidden filtering or compression.
- Dossier bodies are byte-identical to the prose renderer's bodies; the
  assistance-specific citation legend adds labels without creating a second
  summary.
- Eligible-character ordering, citation assignment, and output attachment are
  deterministic.
- Full prose readiness and nonblank `must_render` are not required, while an
  unidentifiable moment, unresolved POV, missing eligible target, or invalid
  dossier authority fails before transport.
- Preview, source disclosure, local edits, keep/copy/clear, navigation, and
  stale marking make no provider call.
- Analyze sends exactly one server-rebuilt inspected prompt only after strict
  capability admission and fingerprint match.
- Structural parser tests cover every quarantine rule, including missing
  characters, wrong counts, duplicate characters, cross-character dossier
  citations, and unknown context citations.
- Route and privacy tests prove zero project mutation, no prompt or result
  logging, no secret leakage, no client-prompt trust, and no provider call on
  every pre-send failure.
- Browser proof covers both entry points, saved-versus-unsaved disclosure,
  accessible controls and status messages, Prompt Inspector, explicit Analyze,
  grouped cards, keyboard operation, keep/copy/clear, per-character regenerate,
  stale recovery, session reload, and zero automatic brief mutation.
- A bounded real-model evaluation exercises ensemble dialogue, materially
  active silent presence, physical or practical interaction, and
  secret/relationship pressure through the configured in-app OpenRouter path.
- The evaluation records every slate's target completeness, dossier
  specificity, behavioral distinctness, immediate feasibility, meaningful
  local effect, continuity/reveal safety, and absence of scene prose or plot
  planning. Any critical source, safety, prose, plot-planning, or missing-target
  failure blocks completion. Character specificity and distinctness failures
  require prompt refinement and replay rather than parser heuristics.
- `npm run lint`, `npm run typecheck`, `npm test`, and `npm run build` pass.

## Rejected Or No-Op Alternatives

- **Use Author Focus inside Ideate:** rejected because focus cannot change
  operator slots, grounding bundles, or target coverage.
- **Add a character mode to Ideate:** rejected for the first version because
  character-indexed complete coverage and pressure-operator ideation have
  different assignment and output contracts.
- **Embed the universal prose prompt wholesale:** rejected because its role,
  prose-craft, invention, stop, and final-output instructions ask for prose
  rather than advisory alternatives.
- **Create a new compressed character summary:** rejected because active/full
  dossiers are already binding authority and must not gain a competing
  assistant-authored representation.
- **Ask for dialogue samples:** rejected because the surface exists to broaden
  agency beyond one or two phrases and must not generate scene prose.
- **Generate ensemble packages:** rejected because combinations become branches
  or scene plans and create an exponential compatibility problem.
- **Include present-minor or offstage cast automatically:** rejected because it
  overrides the author's cast-band declaration and invites material action from
  compressed or relevance-only authority.
- **Read unsaved Generation Brief form state:** rejected because the server
  cannot rebuild it as stored source and hidden UI input would break prompt
  inspection and fingerprint truth.
- **Require full prose readiness:** rejected because the workflow should help
  the author refine `must_render`.
- **Prefill chosen cards into `must_render`:** rejected because Cast Member
  draft import is the sole assistance-output prefill exception and the author
  must independently write canonical brief content.
- **Persist cards in the project:** rejected because suggestions are ephemeral,
  non-canonical scratch and must not become a hidden future prompt source.
- **Parser-enforce literary distinctness with keywords:** rejected because
  brittle language heuristics create false confidence; structural rules and
  human field evaluation own different claims.
- **Publish the entire Causally Due Developments program now:** rejected because
  this session selected and resolved only the Entity-responses slice.
- **Create a glossary entry or ADR:** no-op. The workflow's active spec,
  compiler contract, and PRD are the appropriate durable owners.
