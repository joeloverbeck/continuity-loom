## 1. Executive decision summary

The playtest method already has unusually strong custody, visibility, prompt-inspection, author-authority, correction, and structural-report controls. Its important remaining gaps are not more browser automation, more report fields, or a larger usability framework. They are three narrow evidence problems:

1. the instructed operator is not genuinely cold to the product, and the same operator also selects and interprets the evidence;
2. a conditional one-retry rule does not consistently expose instability in decision-driving stochastic assistance; and
3. method-level learning remains trapped in story-local reports and Git archaeology, with no privacy-safe cross-run view of coverage, recurrence, witnesses, or landings.

The coherent response is **three compact controls**, not five independent procedures.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **MG-02 — Predeclared Paired-Draw Instability Check** | Stochastic prompt evaluation and uncertainty; model-execution provenance | **Adopt now, subject to one prospective witness** | One additional cold subagent response on at most one qualifying assistance prompt per run; roughly minutes | Next naturally triggered, decision-driving Segment Reconciliation prompt |
| 2 | **MG-01 — Independent Witness Gate** | Staged blindness; instruction contamination; independent claim-evidence and rival-explanation review | **Adopt now, subject to one bounded trial** | One short cold review on qualifying runs; capped at the first screen plus three claim packets | Next new-story run, or next run with a decision-driving finding if no new-story run occurs first |
| 3 | **MG-03 — Cross-Run Method Register** | Natural-task coverage; non-saturation coverage accounting; method signals and revision triggers | **Adopt now as a three-run pilot** | One privacy-safe row per run, added after report publication; roughly minutes | Retrospective encoding of the two existing runs plus the next natural run |

“Adopt now” here is a research disposition under the commission’s contract, **not an adoption decision**. The owner must still verify the citations, adjudicate the recommendation, run its witness, and land any accepted revision through the normal skill process.

Several tempting alternatives should be rejected: a full assurance case, a formal qualitative-confidence framework, numerical confidence scoring, inter-rater coefficients, routine five-or-more response sampling, best-of-N selection, a forced combinatorial task matrix, a fixed saturation threshold, a semantic “truth validator,” a second full journey, or a participant panel. They are either already covered in functional terms, cross a settled fence, overstate what this evidence can establish, or impose ceremony disproportionate to a single-maintainer playtest.

This bounded audit does not establish that no further method gaps exist.

### 3.2 What is already strong

The method should not be “improved” by replacing its strongest parts. The following are mature enough for the present envelope:

- deterministic local custody and provider prevention;
- visible-UI-only ecological discipline;
- exact prompt extraction without durable raw-prompt publication;
- cold-context isolation rather than parent-context simulation;
- explicit human editing and acceptance;
- fact/interpretation/correction separation;
- surface-specific adoption and burden ledgers;
- privacy-safe evidence retention and cleanup; and
- structural, custody, privacy, evidence-link, and counterfactual-disclosure validation.

The method’s weak frontier is semantic and longitudinal: **who is cold, who challenges the claim, how stochastic discordance is exposed, and how method evidence accumulates without importing story content.**

### 3.3 Empirical witnesses from the two runs

The new-story run lasted about 96 minutes, carried all 22 deliberately populated brief fields into the visible prose prompt, completed one accepted segment, and observed a byte-identical Segment Reconciliation prompt produce first a structurally valid empty result and then ten useful deltas. [I13]

The continuation lasted about 80 minutes, restored the same project through a fresh isolated process, carried 23 populated fields, and observed the materially smaller reconciliation prompt again produce a complete false-negative no-op followed by a byte-identical retry with 12 brief replacements and ten record-field edits. The report correctly described this as repetition of a failure mode, not a statistical rate estimate. [I15]

The downstream PRD-prep artifact also narrowed an earlier report explanation: prompt highlighting existed, while navigation to matches was missing. That does not invalidate the observed friction; it shows that one operator’s causal or implementation interpretation can be too broad and still become durable unless challenged. The same artifact identified the first report’s missing counterfactual disclosure, which later became an organic method repair. [I14] [I16]

These are the decisive local witnesses for MG-01, MG-02, and MG-03.

## 4. Candidate-area findings and recommendation contracts

## 4.1 Area 1 — Staged blindness and instruction-contamination control

### Finding

**A consequential gap survives.** The method’s “blind-first” rule protects against source code, tests, APIs, hidden state, and prior unrelated reports, but it does not make the operator blind to the skill’s product-specific surface map, purposes, expected coverage, authority model, or completion path. [I1]

A pre-use mental-model entry records what the instructed operator expects; it does not reconstruct what a genuinely uninstructed first-time author would infer. The strongest warranted current claim is therefore “first use by an instructed but source-blind operator,” not “unaided discoverability for a cold author.”

Cognitive walkthrough methods are explicitly concerned with exploratory learning and first-time use without formal training, but a walkthrough is still an evaluator method, not a substitute for real users. [E1] Blinding evidence from experimental research shows a general risk when outcome assessment involves judgment and the assessor knows information capable of influencing that judgment; its transfer here is an analogy, not clinical evidence about software agents. [E4]

### Disposition

**Adopt now through MG-01’s first-view phase, subject to a bounded witness.** Do not rewrite the whole journey as a double-blind study and do not claim human transfer. The smallest useful control is a sealed cold first-view witness on the already-captured unopened state.

## 4.2 Area 2 — Independent claim-evidence and rival-explanation review

### Finding

**A consequential gap survives.** The current schema separates observation from interpretation and allows corrections, but the same agent chooses what to inspect, what counts as evidence, the likely product layer, severity, confidence, and report wording. The downstream narrowing of the prompt-search explanation demonstrates the practical failure mode.

This is not merely theoretical. In an HCI study where four evaluators analyzed the same four usability-test sessions, only 20% of 93 unique problems were found by all four and 46% were found by only one; a substantial evaluator effect remained even for severe problems. [E2] A later study with 19 experienced professionals found a substantial evaluator effect in both moderated and unmoderated sessions and notable disagreement about severity. [E3] These studies do **not** prove that a second evaluator is correct; they show that a single evaluator is an unstable observation instrument and that disagreement itself is information.

Qualitative-method guidance likewise treats reflexivity, negative cases, alternative explanations, audit trails, and triangulation as ways to expose how interpretations were produced rather than as mechanical guarantees of truth. [E5] [E6] [E7] The software-test oracle literature reinforces the limit: for many outputs, distinguishing correct from incorrect behavior is itself a difficult oracle problem. [E10]

### Disposition

**Adopt now through MG-01’s claim-challenge phase, subject to a bounded witness.** Review only claims capable of driving a material decision; do not double-code the entire report, compute inter-rater reliability, or ask a second model to vote on “truth.”

## 4.3 Recommendation MG-01 — Independent Witness Gate

### Gap

The method lacks both (a) a knowledge-exposure control for first-view discoverability claims and (b) an independent semantic challenge for the small number of claims that drive backlog, policy, or method decisions.

### Consequence

The skill cannot reliably distinguish:

- unaided discoverability from behavior enabled by reading the skill;
- a direct visible fact from an overbroad causal or implementation explanation;
- a robust decision-driving finding from one evaluator’s selection or severity judgment; or
- a genuinely preserved strength from a favorable interpretation that ignored a negative case.

### Current coverage

Pre-use intent, first-screen inspection, visible-only action, contemporaneous notes, fact/interpretation separation, corrections, confidence labels, bounded diagnostics, and downstream PRD prep all reduce error. They do not create an evaluator with a different knowledge state, and they do not challenge the report before publication. Structural validation cannot close this gap. [I1] [I3] [I8] [I16]

### Prior art

- **Direct HCI evidence:** evaluator effect studies show materially different problem sets and severity judgments from evaluators analyzing the same sessions. [E2] [E3]
- **HCI method function:** cognitive walkthroughs focus on first-time exploratory learning, giving a functional basis for a cold first-view check. [E1]
- **Cross-domain control analogy:** blinding is most relevant where outcome assessment involves judgment that could be influenced by known information. [E4]
- **Qualitative-method function:** reflexivity, audit trails, negative cases, rival explanations, and triangulation make the inferential route inspectable. [E5]–[E7]
- **Contrary limit:** a second evaluator can disagree or share the same bias; assurance structure and independent review do not certify content quality. [E3] [E9]

### Adapted instrument

Add one **Independent Witness Gate** on qualifying runs. It is one bounded review with two sealed phases.

**Phase A — Cold first view, new-story runs only**

1. Before the main operator’s first navigation, retain one privacy-safe screenshot of the unopened app state. It must not contain story prose, prompts, raw responses, record payloads, secrets, or browser-profile data.
2. A genuinely fresh subagent receives only:
   - a one-sentence neutral premise such as “You are an author opening a local fiction-authoring application for the first time”; and
   - the screenshot or tightly scoped visible-state rendering.
3. It records, without repository access or product instructions:
   - what it thinks the screen is for;
   - the first action it would take and why;
   - the expected consequence of that action;
   - terms or destinations it cannot understand or find; and
   - categorical confidence: `clear`, `partly clear`, or `unclear`, with an explanation.
4. Freeze that response before giving the witness any report claim or product-specific method text.

This is a **first-view witness**, not a full cognitive walkthrough, usability test, or human-author simulation.

**Phase B — Claim challenge, qualifying completed or blocked runs**

1. Select at most three claims before publication. A claim qualifies if it is:
   - a blocker or major finding;
   - named as decision-driving in the executive assessment;
   - a likely-layer/causal inference intended to drive product work; or
   - a strength whose preservation would constrain a change.
2. Give the independent witness a privacy-safe packet containing only:
   - claim ID and exact proposed claim wording;
   - observed visible fact;
   - expected versus actual;
   - reproduction steps;
   - cited artifact identity or sanitized crop;
   - operator interpretation and confidence; and
   - no remedy, raw story material, full prompt, raw response, candidate prose, or accepted prose.
3. Require exactly one status:
   - `supported` — packet warrants the claim as written;
   - `narrowed` — fact is supported but wording/layer/severity exceeds the packet;
   - `contradicted` — packet contains evidence against the claim;
   - `insufficient` — privacy-safe evidence cannot adjudicate it.
4. Require one plausible rival explanation and one observable discriminator. `No credible rival found` is allowed only with a reason.
5. The main operator resolves the challenge transparently. The witness never becomes the authority and cannot auto-delete a finding.

**Evidence-basis tags**

To prevent the existing confidence label from carrying more meaning than it can support, add one or more non-numeric basis tags to decision-driving claims:

`direct-visible`, `reproduced`, `source-confirmed`, `independent-supported`, `independent-narrowed`, `independent-contradicted`, `paired-concordant`, `paired-discordant`, `cross-run-recurrent`, `counterfactual-suggestive`, `single-observer-inference`.

These describe how the claim is supported; they do not calibrate severity or probability.

### Assumptions and limits

The instrument assumes a fresh subagent can be created without inheriting the parent conversation and that a privacy-safe first screen or claim packet remains intelligible. It should work best for discoverability, wording, visible workflow, and causal overreach. It is weaker for subtle story-specific grounding because privacy-safe evidence may be insufficient.

It cannot establish human-author transfer, accessibility, population prevalence, calibrated severity, statistical reliability, or true independence when reviewer and operator use related model families. A screenshot does not test interaction. Persistent disagreement must be routed to verdict calibration or additional product diagnostics, not settled by majority vote.

| One-time maintainer work | Tens of minutes to define packet and report rows; possibly low single-digit hours if validator shape checks are added |
| Recurring maintainer time | Roughly 10–20 minutes on a qualifying run; zero on runs with no qualifying claims and no new-story first view |

### Cheapest first witness

On the next new-story run:

1. capture the unopened first screen;
2. obtain one cold first-view record;
3. challenge up to three decision-driving claims before publication; and
4. record whether each challenge changed claim wording, layer, severity, evidence, or confidence.

**Evidence for adoption:** the witness catches or narrows at least one material claim, identifies a first-view issue hidden by instructions, or materially increases warrant at a tolerable cost without privacy leakage.

**Evidence against adoption:** the cold witness is consistently contaminated, adds no discriminating information, forces unsafe disclosure, or consumes substantial time without changing the support or wording of any qualifying claim. One null trial is not proof of no value, but it is sufficient reason not to make the gate universal until another decision-driving case arises.

### Risks and dependencies

- **Correlated model bias:** same-family subagents may agree for the wrong reason. Mitigation: call it an independent *context* witness, not an independent human rater; retain rival explanations.
- **False assurance:** `independent-supported` can be misread as “true.” Mitigation: basis tags, no score, and explicit limits.
- **Ceremony creep:** reviewing every finding would violate the envelope. Mitigation: maximum three claims and explicit eligibility.
- **Privacy pressure:** a reviewer may need details that cannot be retained. Mitigation: `insufficient` is a valid outcome; never expand the packet past the commission’s custody boundary.
- **Anchoring:** seeing a proposed remedy can bias review. Mitigation: exclude remedies until after status and rival explanation are frozen.
- **Blindness conflict:** Phase B would contaminate Phase A. Mitigation: seal Phase A before Phase B.

### Disposition

**Adopt now, subject to the cheapest witness.** Materiality is established by the instructed-operator design, HCI evaluator effects, and the observed downstream narrowing. Feasibility is high because fresh cold subagents and privacy-safe evidence already exist. The cap prevents a second journey or full double-coding regime.

### Landing target

If later adopted:

- `.claude/skills/playtest/references/run-setup.md` — capture and seal the cold first-view state;
- `.claude/skills/playtest/references/observation-log.md` — claim eligibility, challenge statuses, rival explanation, and basis tags;
- `.claude/skills/playtest/references/report-format.md` — durable disclosure rows;

## 4.4 Area 3 — Stochastic prompt-evaluation design and uncertainty

### Finding

**A consequential gap survives.** The current exact-prompt, fresh-subagent, structured-verdict, retry, and counterfactual machinery is substantial. It already warns that one stochastic response is suggestive rather than proof. [I6] The missing capability is narrower: the retry is triggered only after the operator classifies the first result as materially poor. That is outcome-dependent sampling.

A favorable first output usually ends evaluation, so the method does not learn whether the exact same prompt also produces a valid no-change, malformed, or misleading output. A poor first output receives a second draw and therefore gets more opportunity to reveal variance. This asymmetry can create both false reassurance and false blame.

The local evidence is unusually strong: in both runs, an exact Segment Reconciliation prompt produced a legal all-empty response and then many useful edits on the allowed retry. [I13] [I15] The second occurrence persisted after the prompt shrank materially, weakening prompt length as a sufficient explanation. It still does not estimate a population rate or isolate model, decoding, or host drift.

LLM evaluation research directly warns that single-output evaluation obscures non-determinism. [E11] Research on prompt sensitivity likewise argues that single prompt realizations can be unreliable, but its many-resample, meaning-preserving perturbation design is far larger than this playtest can justify. [E12] The oracle problem means that “take the majority” or “choose the best” does not solve correctness for open-ended continuity work. [E10]

### Recommendation MG-02 — Predeclared Paired-Draw Instability Check

#### Gap

The method has no predeclared, outcome-independent comparator for one naturally triggered, high-impact stochastic assistance prompt, and it does not durably identify the cold executor/model context well enough to interpret cross-run changes.

#### Consequence

The skill cannot reliably distinguish:

- a stable no-change result from one stochastic miss;
- stable usefulness from a lucky first response;
- prompt-contract weakness from model-execution variability;
- cross-run recurrence from a change of model/executor regime; or
- a decision-worthy product signal from a single favorable or unfavorable draw.

#### Current coverage

Exact visible prompt bytes, fingerprints, fresh contexts, structured per-response verdicts, one unchanged-prompt retry after a poor result, field-influence ledgers, one-variable counterfactuals, adoption ledgers, and explicit stochastic caveats are all present. [I6] The gap is not “no retry” or “no uncertainty language.” It is the lack of a **prospectively selected pair whose second draw does not depend on the first outcome**.

#### Prior art

- **Direct LLM-evaluation evidence:** single-output evaluation can miss performance variability created by non-deterministic generation. [E11]
- **Broader sensitivity evidence:** ReliableEval formalizes prompt-phrasing sensitivity and estimates resampling needs, demonstrating why one prompt realization is a weak basis for a reliability claim. [E12]
- **Experimental-design principle:** preregistration separates prediction from postdiction and reduces outcome-contingent analytic choices; the relevant transfer here is a tiny pre-dispatch declaration, not a formal registered study. [E8]
- **Software-testing limit:** open-ended output evaluation suffers from an oracle problem; replication detects discordance but does not automatically reveal which output is correct. [E10]
- **Contrary evidence and fit limit:** benchmark-scale repeated sampling, prompt perturbation, best-of-N selection, and variance estimates require far more draws and a stable metric. They are disproportionate here and would alter the exact-prompt question.

#### Adapted instrument

Pilot a fixed two-draw check on **at most one naturally triggered assistance prompt per run**.

**Eligibility — declare before first dispatch**

A prompt qualifies when all are true:

1. it arises naturally in the one-segment author journey;
2. its result could materially change continuity state, author work, or a decision-driving report finding;
3. a structurally valid no-change or empty result is possible; and
4. the operator can define informative output classes without seeing a response.

The first pilot should be **Segment Reconciliation**, because both local runs provide a direct witness. Prose generation is excluded from the first pilot: forcing a second prose candidate would change the author-choice and intervention-burden path. Ideate and Record Hygiene remain eligible for later extension only if a natural run demonstrates comparable consequence.

**Procedure**

1. Before dispatch, record:
   - prompt kind and fingerprint;
   - why it qualifies;
   - observable classes expected to be informative, such as `substantive-change`, `reasoned-no-change`, `empty-no-change`, `malformed`, or `blocked`;
   - cold-executor host/family and model identifier if exposed;
   - whether decoding configuration is exposed; and
   - a fixed rule: exactly two fresh contexts on byte-identical prompt bytes.
2. Dispatch draw A and draw B independently. Do not tell B about A. Use the existing retry allowance as draw B for this qualifying prompt; do not add an outcome-triggered third retry.
3. Assess both raw responses separately before choosing anything to re-author.
4. Classify the pair:
   - `concordant-substantive`;
   - `concordant-no-change`;
   - `discordant`;
   - `both-poor-or-malformed`; or
   - `blocked`.
5. Record the author’s action separately. The author may work from either response, both, or neither, but selection is not evidence that the selected output was more probable or “the model’s answer.”
6. When the pair is discordant:
   - lower the warrant of any model-reliability or prompt-contract claim based only on the pair;
   - preserve both verdicts and the exact-prompt fingerprint;
   - use source evidence, visible state, an allowed one-variable counterfactual, or recurrence in a later natural run before assigning a product layer; and
   - never report a rate, percentage, or general model reliability estimate.
7. The existing targeted counterfactual remains separate. It may be used only under its current eligibility rule and is not a tie-breaker.

**Durable provenance fields**

Record, without raw prompt or response content:

- `cold_executor_host` (`claude`, `codex`, or current equivalent);
- `cold_executor_model` (exact exposed identifier or `unknown`);
- `cold_executor_model_exposed` (`true`/`false`);
- attempt timestamps;
- prompt fingerprint;
- pair class; and
- separate verdict/adoption outcome for each draw.

This provenance is necessary for interpreting future recurrence. It is not a request for hidden platform internals; `unknown` is an honest value.

#### Assumptions and limits

The check assumes the host can create two genuinely fresh contexts and preserve byte-identical prompt input. Two draws are enough to expose one discordance, not to estimate its probability. Concordance does not establish stability; discordance does not identify the cause. The result is model-, host-, date-, prompt-, and story-conditioned.

The instrument cannot calibrate usefulness labels, compare provider parsing/result cards, estimate model quality, or distinguish all prompt/source/model interactions. Those remain separately routed.

| Compute/tooling | One additional cold subagent response compared with a favorable single-draw path; no OpenRouter/provider call |
| Participants | None |
| Dependencies | Fresh-context mechanism, exact-prompt file custody, temporary raw-response storage, separate per-draw verdicts |

#### Cheapest first witness

On the next naturally triggered Segment Reconciliation prompt:

1. declare eligibility and output classes before dispatch;
2. obtain exactly two byte-identical cold draws regardless of draw A quality;
3. classify the pair and compare the resulting report claim with what the old conditional rule would have supported; and
4. record incremental time and whether model identity was available.

The two historical reports establish plausibility but do not replace this prospective witness because the second draw there was outcome-triggered.

**Evidence for adoption:** the fixed pair reveals consequential discordance that the old rule could have missed after a favorable first draw; or concordance materially strengthens the narrow prompt-instance claim at trivial cost.

**Evidence against adoption:** the second draw adds no decision-relevant information across the prospective witness and a later qualifying case, or its cost/handling materially disrupts the author journey. Even then, retain explicit model provenance if feasible.

#### Risks and dependencies

- **Pseudo-statistics:** two draws can tempt rate language. Mitigation: categorical pair class and an explicit ban on percentages or reliability estimates.
- **Best-of-two bias:** choosing the more useful output can be mistaken for evaluation. Mitigation: assess both first; separate author selection from reliability inference.
- **Prompt drift:** accidental byte differences destroy the comparator. Mitigation: one fingerprint and immutable prompt file for both draws.
- **Model drift:** an opaque host may change models. Mitigation: record exposed identity or `unknown` and date; do not merge incomparable runs silently.
- **Extra compute:** every prompt should not be doubled. Mitigation: one naturally triggered, predeclared high-impact assistance prompt at most.
- **Human-authority erosion:** repeated outputs may pressure adoption. Mitigation: neither output enters canonical state directly; the author may reject both.

#### Disposition

**Adopt now, subject to one prospective witness.** This has the strongest local materiality, the lowest recurring cost, and a direct fit with the existing retry infrastructure. Reject any expansion into a benchmark suite unless later evidence and a separate commission justify it.

#### Landing target

If later adopted:

- `.claude/skills/playtest/references/prompt-evaluation.md` — eligibility, pair procedure, classifications, provenance, and limits;
- `.claude/skills/playtest/references/report-format.md` — per-draw rows and pair disclosure;
- `.claude/skills/playtest/scripts/validate-report.mjs` and tests — prompt-fingerprint equality, count/class consistency, and required provenance shape only.

## 4.5 Area 4 — Natural-task portfolio coverage and saturation

### Finding

**A narrower gap survives; the saturation hypothesis does not survive as stated.** Each report accurately discloses its single-story and single-viewport limits, and the method forbids forced feature use. That is good. The missing capability is a cross-run view of naturally occurring task conditions, not a requirement to manufacture a representative portfolio.

A literal combinatorial task matrix would conflict with sincerity and could turn the author journey into coverage theater. NIST’s combinatorial-coverage work supports the limited idea that existing tests can be characterized by the input-space combinations they contain, but its quantitative assurance claims concern engineered test matrices and cannot be imported into two natural author journeys. [E13]

Likewise, qualitative saturation is not a generic report-count threshold. The literature distinguishes multiple forms of saturation, documents uncertainty and inconsistent use, and argues that operationalization must fit the research question, theory, and analytic framework. [E14] Fixed “five-user” folklore is also unsafe: usability samples of the same size can uncover materially different problem sets. [E15]

### Disposition

**Adopt a descriptive coverage register through MG-03; reject a saturation claim, fixed threshold, or forced portfolio.** The register may say `unobserved`, `single witness`, `repeated-similar`, or `varied witness`. It may not say “saturated,” “representative,” or “complete.”

## 4.6 Area 5 — Longitudinal method feedback and revision triggers

### Finding

**A consequential gap survives.** The repository already has an observable normal loop: run, report, downstream prep/triage, method or product change, later retest. The first report’s counterfactual disclosure defect reached a method repair; the continuation explicitly retested earlier findings. [I14]–[I16]

What is absent is a small method-level index that distinguishes:

- method signal from product finding;
- first occurrence from recurrence;
- witness from adoption;
- current disposition from historical report status; and
- a proposed change from its landing and later retest.

W3C PROV provides a broad model for identifying entities, activities, attribution, versioning, procedures, and derivation, but a full PROV implementation would be absurd here. [E16] Plan–Do–Study–Act literature supports explicit small-cycle predictions and learning records, while reviews also show inconsistent application and warn that an oversimplified cycle can substitute ritual for learning. [E17] [E18] The useful transfer is therefore one minimal provenance-and-trigger record, not a quality-management program.

### Disposition

**Adopt now as the three-run MG-03 pilot.** The artifact must be opened only after the journey report is published so it cannot contaminate blind-first or report-driven continuation.

## 4.7 Recommendation MG-03 — Cross-Run Method Register

### Gap

The method lacks one privacy-safe cross-run artifact that records coarse in-envelope coverage, method-level signals, explicit review triggers, witness results, owner dispositions, and eventual landing identities.

### Consequence

The skill cannot efficiently know:

- whether natural runs are repeatedly clustering in the same task conditions;
- whether an apparent method problem is first-seen, recurrent, fixed, or merely rediscovered;
- when recurrence is sufficient to trigger method review rather than another story-local note;
- why a method change was made and what evidence witnessed it; or
- whether a later audit is repeating already rejected or adopted work.

### Current coverage

Within a story chain, stable finding IDs and the cumulative ledger are strong. Git history, PRD prep, skill audit, and normal review can reconstruct method evolution. [I8] [I14]–[I16] They do not provide a target-level, privacy-safe, current index, and the continuation rule correctly prevents scanning unrelated reports during the journey.

### Prior art

- **Coverage analogy:** characterize the input-space conditions present in existing tests before claiming thoroughness; do not infer behavior in unobserved combinations. [E13]
- **Qualitative-method limit:** saturation has multiple meanings and must fit the question and analytic approach; a fixed report threshold is not defensible. [E14]
- **Sample-size contrary evidence:** small usability samples have high variability in problems discovered, undermining a generic “five runs/users” completion rule. [E15]
- **Provenance function:** source identity, attribution, processing, versioning, procedure, and derivation support assessments of reliability and trustworthiness. [E16]
- **Improvement-cycle function and limit:** small iterative cycles benefit from explicit predictions and documented learning, but PDSA is frequently under-specified or ritualized. [E17] [E18]
- **Framework-import warning:** complex qualitative-confidence frameworks are easily misapplied and can create false precision when converted into scores; a 2023 fidelity evaluation found serious misuse in a large share of reviewed applications. [E19]

### Adapted instrument

Create one file, provisionally `reports/playtest-method-register.md`, with two tables. It contains no story premise, prose, full prompts, raw responses, record payloads, or screenshots. It is updated **only after the playtest report has passed final validation and publication**.

#### Table A — Natural-run coverage card

One row per published run:

| Field | Allowed coarse value or rule |
| --- | --- |
| Run/report identity | Existing report path or run ID |
| Run mode | `new_story` / `continuation` |
| Accepted-sequence band | `1` / `2–3` / `4+` |
| Deliberately populated brief-field band | `0–7` / `8–15` / `16+` |
| Selected-record count band | `0–4` / `5–9` / `10+` |
| Naturally arising author need | `establish` / `continue` / `resolve-local-uncertainty` / `other-short-label` |
| Assistance invoked | Any of `ideate`, `record-hygiene`, `segment-reconciliation`; skipped surfaces remain explicit |
| Lifecycle transition | `create→first-accept`, `reopen→next-accept`, or another approved coarse transition |
| Prompt outcome pattern | `all-substantive`, `mixed`, `paired-discordant`, `no-change`, `blocked`, `not-invoked` |
| Candidate intervention | Existing `none` / `light` / `substantial` / `rewrite` / `not-reached` |
| Coverage state | `unobserved`, `single-witness`, `repeated-similar`, or `varied-witness` for the relevant dimension |

The card is descriptive. It must never force a surface into a run merely to fill a cell. It must never claim representativeness, saturation, reliability, or safety for unobserved combinations.

#### Table B — Method signal and landing ledger

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Method signal ID | Stable `M###` identity |
| Source identity | Report/finding, prompt fingerprint, validator output, or commit |
| Method claim affected | The method capability at risk, not the product defect |
| Privacy-safe observation | Short fact without story/prompt/response payload |
| Occurrence state | `first`, `repeated-similar`, `varied-recurrence`, `contradicted`, `resolved-by-witness` |
| Evidence basis | MG-01/MG-02 basis tags |
| Route | `method-review`, `ordinary-skill-defect`, `product`, `verdict-calibration`, `human-transfer`, `policy`, `ood-pressure-test` |
| Trigger | Exact trigger that fired or remains pending |
| Cheapest witness | Named prospective exercise |
| Witness result | `supports`, `mixed`, `against`, `not-run` |
| Owner disposition | `adopted`, `rejected`, `deferred:<trigger>`, `unadjudicated` |
| Landing identity | Commit/path/date or `none` |
| Retest identity | Later report/witness or `none` |

#### Triggers

The register informs review; it never auto-adopts a change.

1. **Immediate ordinary skill-defect route:** any breach of safety, privacy, provider, custody, visible-UI, report-conformance, or executable-guard requirements. Do not wait for recurrence.
2. **Method-review trigger:**
   - the same method signal appears in two independent natural runs; or
   - one decision-driving claim is `narrowed` or `contradicted` by MG-01; or
   - an MG-02 discordance would otherwise be used to justify product or prompt-contract work; or
   - a landed method change fails its declared witness.
3. **Coverage-review trigger:** after three additional natural runs, any declared in-envelope dimension that remains `unobserved`, or repeated clustering that changes confidence in the audit roadmap, prompts an owner decision: recruit/seek a different natural task, explicitly accept the limitation, or commission an out-of-distribution route. It does not authorize forced feature use.
4. **No automatic severity or reliability trigger:** counts alone cannot promote a signal to a product defect or calibrated verdict.

### Assumptions and limits

The register assumes a maintainer will keep one small public artifact current and that coarse dimensions can be recorded without story leakage. It should reduce rediscovery and reveal clustering. It cannot establish coverage completeness, saturation, prevalence, human transfer, or causal validity. Categories remain judgment calls and can be gamed. The three-run pilot includes a sunset decision precisely because a stale register is worse than no register.

The register is not read before or during a story journey. It must not become an alternate continuation report or a global prompt corpus.

### Cost order

| Cost class | Order of magnitude |
| --- | --- |
| One-time maintainer work | Roughly 1–2 hours to reconstruct the two runs, current method repair, and current override identities; less thereafter |
| Recurring maintainer time | Roughly 2–5 minutes per published run plus occasional review when a trigger fires |
| Compute/tooling | None required; optional low-complexity schema/validator later |
| Participants | None |
| Dependencies | Stable report identities, owner adjudication, MG-01/MG-02 basis labels when present |

### Cheapest first witness

1. Retrospectively encode:
   - the new-story run;
   - the continuation;
   - the counterfactual-disclosure method repair; and
   - the two current reference overrides as baseline/conformance events, not adopted external research.
2. Add the next natural run prospectively after report publication.
3. Before looking at the prospective result, state one prediction: “the register will either expose a decision-relevant coverage cluster or materially shorten/alter the next method-review decision.”
4. After the third row, record whether the prediction was supported.

**Evidence for adoption:** the register reveals a material untouched in-envelope condition, prevents a rediscovery, makes a trigger explicit, or changes the owner’s revision/audit decision.

**Evidence against adoption:** no decision changes, the artifact becomes stale or duplicates report/Git data, or updating it requires reading sensitive/unrelated story content. At the pilot sunset the owner must keep, revise, or reject it; “continue indefinitely” is not the default.

### Risks and dependencies

- **Coverage gaming:** dimensions can invite box-ticking. Mitigation: natural use remains controlling; the register is descriptive only.
- **False saturation:** repeated similar rows can look complete. Mitigation: prohibited terms and four non-saturation states.
- **Privacy leakage:** cross-run aggregation can become a story index. Mitigation: coarse bands, report identity only, no semantic story dimensions.
- **Contamination:** reading the register could shape first-use behavior. Mitigation: post-publication update only.
- **Maintenance debt:** a stale register can mislead. Mitigation: three-run sunset and explicit `last_updated`/owner field.
- **Framework creep:** provenance or quality-improvement vocabulary can expand. Mitigation: two tables only; no PROV graph, PDSA program, or confidence framework.

### Disposition

**Adopt now as a three-run pilot.** It closes both surviving longitudinal gaps with one lightweight artifact and a falsifiable sunset. Its value depends on owner use, so permanent adoption should wait for the pilot witness.

### Landing target

If later adopted:

- `reports/playtest-method-register.md` — sole cross-run artifact;
- `.claude/skills/playtest/references/report-format.md` — post-publication update rule and privacy boundary;
- optionally `.claude/skills/playtest/SKILL.md` — one sentence naming method-review triggers and prohibiting pre-journey register reading.

### 5.2 Additional surviving gap: stochastic-executor provenance

The current report frontmatter records cold attempt counts but not a durable exact model/executor identity. [I8] Because LLM behavior can vary by model, decoding regime, and time, cross-run recurrence cannot be interpreted cleanly without at least the identity exposed by the host. This is consequential but too small for a fourth recommendation. It is incorporated into MG-02’s per-draw provenance and MG-03’s method-signal row.

**Disposition:** **Adopt as part of MG-02/MG-03.** Record exact exposed identity or `unknown`; never infer hidden platform details.

### 5.3 No defensible additional gap found in these classes

- **More safety/custody instrumentation:** already strong and executable. No evidence supports duplicating it.
- **Raw browser traces or full prompt/response archives:** they would materially worsen privacy and contradict the evidence boundary.
- **Automatic semantic report validation:** the oracle problem and assurance-standard limits make this a false-assurance risk. Automated checks may validate shape, hashes, counts, and declared relationships, not whether a claim is true. [E9] [E10]
- **Full reflexive journal:** the scratchpad, fact/interpretation separation, and correction entries already supply the useful function. MG-01 adds only the missing independent challenge.
- **Full qualitative confidence framework:** disproportionate and domain-incongruent. CERQual applies to qualitative evidence syntheses, not one mixed-method agent playtest; published fidelity problems warn against casual borrowing and numerical scoring. [E19]
- **Formal inter-rater reliability:** two non-independent model contexts and categorical, context-bound findings do not support a meaningful coefficient.
- **Benchmark-scale stochastic evaluation:** incompatible with the time envelope and exact natural task. MG-02 is deliberately a discordance detector, not a benchmark.
- **Metamorphic prompt suite:** changing prompt wording would answer a different question from exact compiled-prompt behavior and could exceed the one-counterfactual rule.
- **Participant panel:** human transfer calibration is separately fenced.

## 7. Conflicts and dependencies among the proposed instruments

| Conflict or dependency | Resolution |
| --- | --- |
| MG-01 first-view blindness versus later claim review | Freeze Phase A before giving the witness any claim packet or method text. A host without thread continuation may use two separately sealed fresh contexts under one capped review. |
| MG-01 review versus privacy | Sanitize aggressively. `insufficient` is preferable to requesting story prose, full prompts, raw responses, or record payloads. |
| MG-01 reviewer agreement versus authority | Agreement is an evidence basis, not a vote. The author/operator remains responsible for wording and route. |
| MG-02 repeated outputs versus human gatekeeping | Both outputs remain quarantined. The author may use either, both, or neither and must re-author canonical changes visibly. |
| MG-02 paired draw versus counterfactual | The pair holds prompt bytes constant; the counterfactual changes one variable. Keep them distinct, separately disclosed, and never use the counterfactual as a stochastic third vote. |
| MG-02 model provenance versus unavailable platform metadata | Record `unknown`; do not infer. Unknown provenance lowers cross-run comparability but does not block the run. |
| MG-03 coverage accounting versus naturalism | Record what occurred; never select a surface solely to improve the register. Coverage gaps inform later recruitment/task choice or explicit limitation. |
| MG-03 register versus report-driven continuation | Open and update the register only after the report is published. It is never a source for navigating or interpreting the active story journey. |
| Validator expansion versus semantic warrant | Validators may enforce row presence, enums, hashes, pair counts, and cross-references. They must not output “claim supported” or calculate a confidence score. |
| Combined recurring ceremony | The design is intentionally capped: one extra draw, one independent review on qualifying runs, one post-report row. If routine duration rises materially, preserve MG-02 first, narrow MG-01 eligibility, and revisit MG-03 at its sunset. |

Dependency order is therefore: define MG-02 and MG-01 row semantics first; then let MG-03 index their outputs. No recommendation depends on recruiting participants or changing product behavior.
