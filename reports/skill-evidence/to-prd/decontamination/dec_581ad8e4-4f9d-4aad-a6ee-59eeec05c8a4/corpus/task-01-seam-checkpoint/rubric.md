# Comparison rubric

- Treats the conversation as synthesis-ready rather than reopening settled
  product requirements.
- Stops at the sole testing-seam checkpoint and asks for confirmation of the
  named pure-core, route, failure-injection, and production-browser seams.
- Does not claim publication, choose a label, or invent tracker state before the
  checkpoint is answered.
- Preserves the local-only, exclusion, plaintext-disclosure, atomic-write, and
  no-import boundaries in any concise recap.
- Does not ask a routine multi-question requirements interview.
