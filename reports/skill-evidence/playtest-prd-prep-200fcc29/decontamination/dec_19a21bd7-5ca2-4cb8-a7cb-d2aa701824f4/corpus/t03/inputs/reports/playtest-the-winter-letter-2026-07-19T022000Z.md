## Executive Assessment

Continuity Loom successfully carried a complex, sensitive time-travel premise from an empty local project to one restrained, causally complete prose segment without treating generated prose as automatic canon. The strongest product behavior was the authority boundary: explicit records and brief fields compiled visibly, manual candidate provenance remained clear, acceptance triggered a durable-change reminder, and lifecycle validation forced the saved brief from `First segment` to `Continuation after accepted segment` before another preview could be considered coherent.

The main product cost was front-loaded authoring effort. The local-folder first step assumes filesystem fluency; Story Configuration uses schema-shaped labels; each required CAST MEMBER dossier demanded more than twenty authored values plus guardrail lists; and the Working Set obscured cast identity by labeling rows with dossier descriptions rather than names. These costs were recoverable but material for a first story.

Prompt behavior was strong in the prose and Record Hygiene cases. Segment Reconciliation showed a serious instance-level variance: one cold draw returned no changes and a literal fingerprint placeholder, while the paired draw returned eleven well-cited deltas. That pair does not establish a failure rate, but it does show why an empty or provenance-invalid reconciliation result needs validation or an explicit no-change justification before an author relies on it.

## Story Intent and Expectations

The intended story was a restrained speculative drama about Tomás Vidal, a Spanish man born in 1985 who is the sole time traveler and has lived roughly two thousand subjective years. In 2006, Daniel Hale asks Tomás to prevent the 1972 suicide of Daniel's younger sister Clara, a mathematically gifted and socially isolated woman who believes Daniel's coma is permanent. The first segment was deliberately limited to Tomás entering Clara's Wisconsin sanatorium room on Christmas Eve 1972, presenting bounded evidence, and earning only a reversible postponement.

The sealed expectations were:

- close-third past through Tomás, with age expressed as patience rather than exposition;
- Clara's precision and agency expressed through inference and exact questions, without diagnostic certainty or savant caricature;
- Daniel offstage as the source of pressure and evidence;
- the half-written letter, room, corridor, staff, snow, and later woods route kept physically coherent;
- no coercion, romance, wealth pitch, future-technology spectacle, waking date, discharge, hospital scene, or completed rescue;
- a stopping point where Clara asks for proof or chooses another reversible step.

### F001 — Local project path semantics are underexplained

- **Observed fact:** The unopened screen requested Parent path, Folder name, Title, and Description, with no path example, folder chooser, or visible permission explanation. Create/Open purpose was clear. The independent witness counted six unclear terms or concepts; the operator counted three.
- **Interpretation and impact:** A prepared or technically comfortable author can proceed, but a first-time local author may know the intended action without knowing what valid path to supply.
- **Expected versus actual:** Expected a clear local-project choice plus enough guidance to form a valid path; only the choice was self-explanatory.
- **Visible reproduction:** Open the app with no project, inspect the Create Project card before using help, and look for a path example or chooser.
- **Evidence:** [Sealed first view](assets/playtest-the-winter-letter-2026-07-19T022000Z/001-first-view.png); independent first-view table above.
- **Workaround / cost:** Supply a prepared absolute parent path. Cost was minor in this run but depends on prior filesystem knowledge.
- **Likely layer:** UI copy and onboarding.
- **Desired outcome:** Preserve the direct Create/Open fork while adding a short path example, local-folder explanation, or chooser.
- **Uncertainty:** This was one technical operator plus one cold comprehension witness, not a human usability study.
