### Sealed story intent

Render one restrained evidentiary exchange immediately after Clara has postponed
action until the next staff round and requested three Daniel-specific facts.
Remain in Clara's sanatorium room on Christmas Eve 1972, in close-third past
through Tomás. Tomás and Clara are the only onstage people. Daniel remains
physically offstage, comatose in 1972, and present only as pressure and the
claimed source of the facts.

Before app launch, seal these three author-chosen claims as the material Tomás
says Daniel supplied. They are new to this unit and must not repeat the
previously used childhood-cache detail:

1. Daniel once hid a chipped black chess knight in the flour tin after blaming
   Clara for losing it.
2. On a family postcard Daniel crossed out the printed lake and wrote in the
   margin, "The weather is a liar."
3. After their mother overheard a childhood signal, Daniel and Clara replaced
   the phrase "north window" with "second lantern."

These details are author decisions for this story unit, not conclusions the
system may elaborate. They may show that Tomás has a credible path to Daniel.
They do not prove time travel, prove exclusive access, establish Daniel's
eventual recovery, or establish a waking date. Clara should be able to name or
probe alternate source paths such as relatives, papers, correspondence, or a
contemporary intermediary. Do not let the prompt or prose strengthen the facts
into "only Daniel could know" unless Clara herself can support that narrower
claim, and do not require her to accept it.

### Physical, knowledge, voice, and content bounds

- Tomás writes all three facts on his own paperwork using a pencil Clara lends
  him. Clara retains her letter and controls the pencil loan, physical distance,
  room access, pace, and outcome of the exchange.
- Clara tests each claim for specificity and plausible source path. Her precision
  appears through exact questions and inference, without diagnostic certainty,
  caricature, forced belief, or loss of agency.
- Tomás's long experience appears as patience and willingness to concede
  alternative explanations, not exposition, superiority, coercion, or a demand

- Keep staff and the next round as offstage time pressure. Do not move either
  character out of the room, begin rescue/discharge/hospital/mansion logistics,
  resolve Daniel's coma, show Daniel bodily, promise his recovery, supply a waking
  date, or turn the exchange into proof of time travel.
- Stop at a new reversible choice made by Clara before staff pressure displaces
  the conversation. Her commitment may extend only to the next local evidentiary
  step, never to rescue, belief, survival, or a future plan.

## Cold Prompt Evaluations

### Exact custody and isolation

For each naturally invoked surface, first write down the author need and expected

Assess the untouched prose for contract compliance, grounding, invention,

## Blocker and Recovery Rules

On a probable blocker, first record the visible state, intended action,
expectation, and last successful action; capture privacy-safe evidence; then
inspect only existing console/network/guard streams and, after recording the
visible issue, the relevant accessibility tree, geometry, or visible DOM
fragment. Try one safe visible recovery, reload once only if state should
persist, and then make at most one fresh guarded-browser attempt against the same
app and project. Never restart the app, replace/reconstruct the project, weaken
validation, randomly click, use hidden state, or silently omit the original
failure.

If any provider send control is clicked or the guard records a provider request,
stop immediately, confirm the guard abort and blank credential, and report
`status: blocked` with `completion_reason: provider-request-attempt`. Do not
retry. Other terminal reasons include `continuation-project-missing`,

kebab-case blocker for an unrecoverable visible app/browser/acceptance failure.
Use `Not reached — <specific blocker>` for later phases, but keep the complete
report shape.
