# T01 Operator Packet — New Sensitive Opening

## Trial disposition

This is an instruction-level packet for a future visible playtest. It does not
assert that an app or browser was launched, a project was created, a prompt was
extracted, a cold response was produced, or a segment was accepted during this
trial. The executable run must replace every receipt-derived placeholder with
the exact value emitted by the run-preparation helper and must report only what
was actually observed.

Quantitative observation tracking is inactive: T01 asks for no action count,
field count, promotion cost, or other quantitative comparison.

## 1. Run charter

### Run identity and boundary

- Task: `T01-new-sensitive-opening`
- Mode: `new_story`
- Working title: **Wet Stone, Still Sword**
- Prior report: none
- Intended accepted-segment count: exactly one
- Segment start: Hina Mori is conscious on wet shrine stone.
- Exact segment end: Hina deliberately agrees to help and grips the ceremonial
  sword while it is still mounted.
- Do not pass the exact end. In particular, do not unmount or activate the
  sword; breach the door; begin combat; identify the outside pressure; explain
  the transfer; or imply that any of those events has occurred.
- Everything after that grip remains undecided and belongs to a later run.

### Story intent

Write a close, immediate opening about disorientation, constrained choice, and
Hina deciding to help an injured stranger under pressure. The scene should make
her agency legible without turning distress or intrusive thoughts into plot
machinery. The local dramatic movement is only from waking, orienting, and
understanding the immediate physical need to an explicit decision and hand on
the still-mounted sword.

### POV, participation, and pressure

- POV: close third person limited to Hina.
- Intended speakers: Hina and Calen only.
- Onstage, physically active people: Hina and Calen.
- Silent/onstage presences: none planned beyond those two people.
- Offstage pressure: an unidentified force acting against the barred door. It
  is pressure, not permission to invent a visible attacker, creature, faction,
  voice, motive, number, or identity.
- Calen is the injured shrine warden and is actively bracing the failing barred
  door. Hina must not acquire knowledge from him that he has not visibly given
  her.
- No absent character becomes present through implication, hallucination,
  telepathy, prophecy, or narration.

### Physical and continuity constraints

- Hina begins conscious, on wet shrine stone.
- Calen begins injured and bracing a barred door that is failing under outside
  pressure.
- A dormant ceremonial sword is mounted within two steps of Hina.
- Preserve the geometry: Hina can reach the mounted sword in two steps, but it
  remains mounted at the stopping point.
- Preserve the door state through the stopping point: strained and failing, but
  not breached.
- Preserve the sword state through the stopping point: dormant, mounted, and
  not activated.
- The segment may establish Hina's immediate perceptions and Calen's directly
  observable condition. It may not establish durable causes, lore, factions,
  magic rules, or identities that the charter leaves undecided.

### Knowledge and reveal locks

- Hina knows her own life in Yokohama and recognizes her own intrusive thoughts
  as unwanted; she does not know Veyr, the transfer cause, the pressure's
  identity, the sword's capabilities, or a larger destiny.
- The narration may name Calen only after Hina can sincerely learn his name in
  the scene or through a visible established record whose use does not violate
  POV knowledge.
- Do not reveal a prophecy, magical mind-reading, a chosen-one role, transfer
  explanation, hidden threat identity, or sword activation.
- Do not let intrusive thoughts predict, cause, summon, control, or accurately
  diagnose external events.

### Voice, prose, and sensitive-content constraints

- Hina is sixteen, Japanese, and from Yokohama. Keep her age, culture, and
  personhood specific without exoticizing or sexualizing her.
- Primarily obsessional OCD is part of her lived experience, not a replacement
  for character. Intrusive thoughts must remain ego-dystonic, non-causal, and
  visibly distinct from desire, intent, knowledge, magic, or moral truth.
- Reject prophecy, magical mind-reading, violence stereotyping, cure-by-isekai,
  destiny shortcuts, sexualization, and diagnostic exposition.
- Avoid presenting violence as inherent to mental illness and avoid treating
  relocation to Veyr as a cure.
- Use controlled sensory prose focused on the wet stone, the door's strain,
  Calen's effort, the reachable sword, and Hina's choice. Do not replace the
  scene with a diagnostic lecture or a fantasy-lore preamble.

### Deliberately unresolved material

- What is outside the door, why it is pressing inward, and whether it is a
  person, creature, crowd, or other force.
- How or why Hina arrived in Veyr.
- Why the sword is dormant, what activates it, and what it can do.
- What happens after Hina grips it.
- Calen's wider history, loyalties, and knowledge beyond the immediate scene.

### Expected author work and mental model

Approach the untouched product as a first-time author expecting to create a
local project, establish the minimum durable story state, select only the
records relevant now, write a bounded generation brief, inspect a compiled
prompt, obtain a cold local prose response without using OpenRouter, edit it in
the visible candidate editor, and accept it once. Expect accepted prose to be
evidence for later author decisions, never automatic continuity authority.

Likely useful durable records, expressed as author needs rather than presumed UI
fields, are:

- Hina's cast dossier, including identity, age, origin, POV role, current
  physical placement, knowledge limits, and the representation constraints
  above;
- Calen's cast dossier, including his role, injury, door-bracing action, and the
  narrow facts Hina may observe or learn;
- the shrine as the current location, including wet stone and the relationship
  among Hina, door, and sword;
- the barred door's current state and unidentified outside pressure;
- the ceremonial sword as mounted, dormant, and within two steps;
- atomic knowledge/reveal boundaries where the visible domain shape supports
  them, without duplicating a rich cast dossier merely to fill fields.

The Active Working Set should include only what the next segment needs: Hina in
the POV/speaker/physically-present functions, Calen in the speaker and active
presence functions, the shrine, door, and sword in their appropriate visible
functions, and the outside force only as offstage pressure. Do not promote the
unknown pressure into cast or canon merely to make it selectable.

Private Notes are not pre-required. Use them only if a genuine disposable
scratch need arises. Any adopted idea must be independently authored into a
canonical record or Generation Brief field. If no such need arises, record the
naturalistic skip: the charter and visible canonical surfaces were sufficient.

### Anticipated sincere assistance needs

- Ideate: before committing the local directive, ask for grounded micro-level
  options or questions about how Hina can orient, respond to Calen, and choose
  to help without extending past the grip. Reject plot branches, threat
  identities, lore, destiny, combat, activation, or transfer explanations.
- Record Hygiene: invoke only if the records visibly create a genuine concern
  about overlap, staleness, or misplaced authority—for example, whether a
  representation constraint, current physical state, and reveal lock have
  distinct durable homes. A useful result may explicitly recommend keeping
  distinct record types distinct; never merge them for apparent tidiness.
- Segment Reconciliation: after acceptance, the segment necessarily creates a
  plausible durable-state review need. Ask only what explicit deltas the
  accepted segment supports. Suggestions remain advisory until the author
  manually applies chosen changes on canonical surfaces.

## 4. Cold prompt evaluations

### Exact custody protocol

For the prose prompt and each assistance prompt that is naturally invoked:

1. Before opening its inspector, record the current author need and the useful
   result expected.
2. With the exact prompt visibly open, record the visible prompt kind, versions,
   scope, counts, readiness, source disclosure, and later its SHA-256
   fingerprint. Extract the complete visible prompt using `text-file` to a
   unique file under `<receipt.exchange_dir>` without printing it.
3. Dispatch exactly one fresh, no-parent-context executor for that attempt. Give
   it only the instruction to read the exact prompt file as its sole task,
   inspect nothing else, and produce only the prompt-requested output. If file
   custody is required, add only the instruction to write the response verbatim
   to the designated response file and return `Response saved.`
4. Do not give the executor this packet, the premise, expected answer, defect
   hypothesis, repository context, prior output, or evaluator preferences.
5. If fresh isolation is unavailable, stop with
   `cold-subagent-unavailable`; do not imitate cold evaluation in the parent.
6. Never inject an assistance response into provider-result UI by API,
   interception, mock, or DOM manipulation. Import only through a visible local
   paste/import flow if that surface explicitly provides one; otherwise manually
   re-author adopted advice in canonical editors.

### Untouched-response assessment

Assess every raw response before any manual rewriting. Record:

- contract compliance;
- grounding versus unsupported invention;
- relevance and actionable output versus generic or no-change output;
- omissions, overreach, contradictions, and false positives;
- adopted and rejected material;
- repair burden;
- likely layer: `prompt contract`, `model execution`, `source data`,
  `UI workflow`, or `not assessable`;
- verdict: `useful`, `mixed`, `low-value`, `misleading`, `malformed`, or
  `blocked`;
- bounded confidence.

For prose, additionally test all of the following against the sealed charter:

- local scope and exact directive;
- wet-stone, door, Calen, sword, and two-step geometry;
- Hina POV and knowledge limits;
- Calen and pressure participation boundaries;
- ego-dystonic, non-causal intrusive thoughts;
- no prophecy, mind-reading, violence stereotyping, cure fantasy,
  sexualization, destiny, or diagnostic replacement of character;
- no unsupported durable fact or future-state invention;
- voice, genericity, content policy, prose quality, and stop discipline;
- final action no later than deliberate agreement plus grip on the
  still-mounted, dormant sword.

Allow one unchanged-prompt prose retry only if the first response is materially
poor or malformed. Use a different fresh executor, provide no critique, assess
both raw responses, and choose the one an author would prefer to edit. If
neither can become acceptable without replacement-level rewriting, stop with
`prose-quality-blocker`.

For each deliberately populated Generation Brief field, search the visible
inspector for the distinctive saved value and label. Record `present`, `absent`,
or `unclear`, plus placement/prominence. After the response, assign exactly one
of `used`, `partly used`, `not compiled`, `not observably used`, `contradicted`,
or `not assessable`, with confidence. Use at most one diagnostic
counterfactual, only if an important visibly compiled field appears ignored and
a one-field comparison could change a product decision; keep that response out
of the app and continuity.

Apply the assistance-specific gates:

- Ideate succeeds only with distinct, grounded, locally usable options or
  questions inside the existing scene. Restatements, disguised branches,
  infeasible options, threat identities, and lore are noise or overreach.
- Record Hygiene must separate actual change proposals from no-change advice,
  avoid unsafe merges, and respect distinct authority types. Record missed
  overlap and whether exhaustive reassurance crowds out action.
- Segment Reconciliation must contrast explicit accepted state deltas with
  current state, paraphrase rather than reproduce prose, use valid visible
  targets/shapes, invent no canon, and make manual work clear. Count adopted,
  rejected, redundant, over-specific, missing, and malformed proposals.

The report must state that this cold path did not exercise provider-response
parsing or provider result cards.

## 7. Bounded recovery and terminal decisions

For an ordinary blocker, record the state and last successful action, preserve
evidence, try one safe visible-control recovery, reload once only if state should
persist, then make at most one fresh guarded-browser attempt against the same
app and project. Do not restart the app or replace the project. On recurrence or
unsafe resumption, stop and report the specific blocker.

Stop immediately, without retry, if a provider send control is clicked or the
provider-block stream gains an entry. Confirm only the guard abort and blank
credential, set `status: blocked` and
`completion_reason: provider-request-attempt`, record the visible action and
endpoint without payload content, and report any uncertainty about escape.

Other honest terminal blockers include app/browser launch failure,
unopenable/unverifiable project, no visible path to required state/readiness/
prompt/candidate/acceptance/sequence proof, repeated crash or state loss,
unavailable cold executor, two unusable prose responses, or state too ambiguous
to continue. Confusion, excessive work, or a poor but repairable response is a
finding rather than automatic completion or blockage.

The run completes only when all of these are true:

- one and only one new accepted segment has a visibly verified positive
  sequence;
- the exact stopping boundary was preserved;
- the candidate burden was recorded;
- post-acceptance continuity was deliberately reviewed and chosen updates were
  made only through canonical visible surfaces;
- zero OpenRouter send controls were clicked, zero provider requests were
  attempted, and zero were blocked;
- the temporary project still exists under `/tmp` for continuation;
- browser and app holders are stopped;
- the report passes validation after private exchange material is removed;
- final repository status differs from the baseline only by the allocated run
  report and cited evidence directory.
