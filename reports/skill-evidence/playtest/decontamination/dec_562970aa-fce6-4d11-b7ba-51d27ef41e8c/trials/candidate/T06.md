# T06 Candidate Operator Packet — Offstage Pressure Only

## Trial disposition

This is an instruction-level simulation packet for a future visible-browser run.
No app, browser, project, provider, prompt executor, or report validator is run

## Run charter

- **Mode:** new story. There is no prior report or continuation project.
- **Working title:** *The Noon Lockout*.
- **Local segment:** Nera Vale, alone with the unmanned tidal-ferry prototype
  before its noon test, discovers and verifies a hairline drive-coupling
  fracture. She refuses the unsafe launch, photographs and tags the defect,
  locks out the drive, and preserves the evidence.
- **Exact stop:** end after the lockout and evidence-preservation actions, before
  Iven Marr appears or any confrontation begins.
- **Still unresolved:** whether Iven arrives in the following segment, how he
  reacts, and what any later confrontation contains. Do not decide these facts
  during this run.
- **Point of view and presence:** close third through Nera; Nera is the sole
  onstage person and the only viewpoint. Iven is a pressure-only offstage person.
  His sole influence in this segment is the already existing stamped paper order
  threatening Nera's certification if she refuses the launch.
- **Iven knowledge boundary:** do not invent his live speech, present location,
  current actions, thoughts, intentions, appearance, biography, relationships,
  or voice. Do not turn the written order into a live message or invent a
  detailed quotation when a paraphrase of its established force suffices.
- **Forbidden presence/content:** no third person, crowd, call, radio voice,
  flashback speaker, injury, passenger peril, romance, or violence.
- **Physical end state sought:** the fracture has been verified; the unsafe
  launch has been refused; the defect is photographed and tagged; the drive is
  locked out; evidence is preserved. These are author-directed outcomes, not
  authority silently derived from generated prose.
- **Voice/prose contract:** restrained close-third industrial suspense, with
  precise physical actions and procedural clarity. Keep the scope local and do
  not use exposition to expand Iven.
- **Expected records:** one durable active dossier for Nera; one minimal
  ENTITY-first representation for Iven; only the smallest additional canonical
  facts or object/current-state records visibly needed to express the prototype,
  the noon test, and the pre-existing stamped order. Do not create an optional
  Iven dossier or fill optional Iven details merely for completeness.
- **Private Notes:** no sincere inert-scratch need is expected, so skip them
  unless an actual temporary author thought arises. Any adopted decision must
  still be authored independently in a canonical record or brief field.
- **Assistance expectation:** Ideate is not expected because the segment's local
  action and stop are already decided. Record Hygiene is not expected because
  the deliberately minimal records have no known overlap or staleness question.
  Post-acceptance Segment Reconciliation is naturally useful because the segment
  changes the prototype's physical/safety state; evaluate it cold if its prompt
  is visibly available, then apply any sound change manually.
- **First-time-author model:** configuration expresses the story/prose contract;
  records hold durable truth; the Working Set scopes the next segment; the
  Generation Brief directs the local prose; the inspector exposes what will be
  sent; a candidate remains editable prose; acceptance archives prose but does
  not make its details canonical.
- **Non-goals:** no provider test, no exhaustive accessibility audit, no second
  segment, no future confrontation, no speculative worldbuilding, no optional
  character enrichment, and no quantitative action/field-count study.

Seal this charter in the allocated scratchpad before opening the app. Later UI
discoveries may correct the operator's mental model, but they may not loosen the
narrative, presence, knowledge, or stop boundaries above.

## Blocker and bounded-recovery rules

A missing visible path to project creation, required canonical state, readiness,
prompt inspection, candidate import, acceptance, sequence proof, or safe manual
post-acceptance update is a probable blocker. So are repeated crash/stall/state
loss, unavailable cold execution, two unusable prose attempts, or ambiguous
project state.

On a probable blocker: record the visible state, intended action, expectation,
and last success; capture minimal privacy-safe evidence; inspect only existing
guard/console/network streams and, after the visible concern is recorded, the
relevant accessibility tree, geometry, or DOM fragment. Try one safe visible
recovery, then one reload only if persistence should be safe, then at most one
fresh guarded-browser attempt against the same app and project. Never restart
the app, replace the project, use a direct API, inspect hidden state, or retry
randomly. On recurrence or unsafe resumption, stop and publish a blocked report.

Any send-control click or provider-block entry ends the run immediately. Confirm
guard abort and blank credentials, record only the visible action and endpoint
without content, use `status: blocked` and
`completion_reason: provider-request-attempt`, and never retry.
