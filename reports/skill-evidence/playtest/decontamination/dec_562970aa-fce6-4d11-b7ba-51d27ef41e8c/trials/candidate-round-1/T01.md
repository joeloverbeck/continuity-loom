# T01 Operator Packet — New Sensitive Opening

## Packet status

This packet specifies a `new_story` browser playtest. It is an instruction-level
simulation only: no app or browser is launched, no project is created or changed,
no prompt is dispatched, and no provider request is made while producing this
packet. In the real run, the operator remains source-and-doc blind and learns the
product only from the visible UI, its labels, and field help.

## Run charter

### Story contract

- Working title: **The Unbidden Oath**.
- Local segment: Hina Mori, a sixteen-year-old girl from Yokohama, regains
  consciousness on wet shrine stone in Veyr. Injured shrine warden Calen is
  bracing a failing barred door against pressure from outside. A dormant
  ceremonial sword remains mounted within two steps of Hina.
- Intended viewpoint: close to Hina's immediate perception. Her knowledge is
  limited to what she can presently perceive, remember, and reasonably infer.
- Onstage participants: Hina and Calen. Calen may speak; Hina may speak or
  respond internally and physically. The outside pressure is offstage and must
  not be identified. No unseen person is promoted to an onstage presence.
- Exact endpoint: Hina deliberately agrees to help and grips the sword while it
  is still mounted. End immediately, before activation, removal, breach, combat,
  threat identification, or an explanation of her displacement.

### Guardrails that must survive authoring and prose

- Hina has primarily obsessional OCD. Intrusive thoughts remain unwanted,
  ego-dystonic, and non-causal: they neither predict nor produce events and are
  not magical information.
- Do not turn intrusive thoughts into prophecy, mind-reading, destiny, a combat
  instinct, or evidence about the pressure outside.
- Do not use violence stereotyping, cure-by-isekai, sexualization of a minor,
  destiny shortcuts, or diagnostic exposition in place of characterization.
- Preserve the physical geometry: wet shrine stone under Hina; Calen at the
  failing barred door; mounted dormant sword within two steps; the grip occurs
  without drawing or activating it.
- Preserve knowledge locks: neither narration nor dialogue may identify the
  outside pressure, explain the transfer, reveal sword powers, or assert that
  Hina is chosen.
- Keep the scene local. Sensory orientation, Calen's visible strain, Hina's
  decision, and the move to the sword are in scope; downstream action is not.

### Deliberately unresolved after this segment

The nature and motive of the outside pressure, whether the door will fail, what
the sword can do, why Hina is in Veyr, whether anyone expected her, and what
happens after she grips the sword all remain undecided. The segment must not
create durable facts about those questions.

### Author model and expected work

The operator acts as a sincere first-time author trying to establish a safe,
specific opening without learning hidden product mechanics. Expected useful
continuity includes Hina and Calen dossiers, the shrine/door situation, the
mounted ceremonial sword, the unidentified outside pressure, Hina's knowledge
and reveal limits, scene geometry, and the sensitive-content constraints.
Create only the records needed for this segment and prefer atomic facts except
where the visible product presents a cast dossier as the natural shape.

Private Notes are not coverage. Use them only if a genuine temporary wording or
planning need arises; anything adopted must be independently authored into a
canonical record or Generation Brief field. The Active Working Set should
include Hina in the visible POV function, Calen in every applicable speaker and
active-presence function, and the outside pressure only in the visible offstage
function. Add the shrine, door condition, and sword only where the UI shows they
are needed for the next segment.

Anticipated sincere assistance needs are:

- Ideate, before committing the local directive, if the author needs several
  grounded ways for Hina to orient, communicate, or reach a decision. Useful
  output supplies local options or questions, not plot branches.
- Record Hygiene, after the small record set exists, if the author needs help
  distinguishing overlap among dossier details, scene facts, knowledge locks,
  object state, and content constraints. Useful output identifies genuine
  authority overlap without merging distinct record types.
- Segment Reconciliation, after acceptance, because the endpoint may explicitly
  change Hina's commitment and physical position. Useful output names only
  explicit durable deltas and remains advisory.

Skip any of these if the corresponding need does not naturally arise, and record
the reason. Do not invoke any additional assistance merely for coverage.

## Cold prompt evaluations

For each naturally reached assistance prompt and for the prose prompt:

1. Before opening the inspector, record the author need and expected useful
   result. With the complete prompt visibly open, save it via the prescribed
   guarded `text-file` action into the run's `/tmp` exchange directory without
   printing it.
2. Give the exact file to one fresh no-parent-context executor with no story
   brief, expected answer, suspected defect, earlier response, or repository
   context. Its sole instruction is to read that complete prompt and perform it;
   when file custody is needed, it writes the response verbatim to the specified
   temporary response file and returns only `Response saved.`
3. Never use OpenRouter, interception, mocks, direct APIs, or injected result
   cards. One fresh executor serves one attempt. If genuine cold isolation is
   unavailable, stop with `cold-subagent-unavailable`.
4. Assess the untouched response before re-authoring: contract compliance,
   grounding, unsupported invention, relevance, omissions, overreach,
   contradictions, actionable versus generic output, adoption/rejection, repair
   burden, likely layer, verdict, and bounded confidence.

Apply these T01-specific checks:

- Ideate options must stay within the shrine's immediate problem and help the
  author choose a local action or question. Reject disguised future branches,
  sword activation, threat identities, transfer explanations, or options that
  exploit Hina's intrusive thoughts as magic.
- Record Hygiene may distinguish or flag overlap, but must not merge a cast
  dossier with knowledge locks, scene state, object state, content constraints,
  or other visibly distinct authority. Separate real changes from no-change
  reassurance and record false positives or missed overlap.
- Prose must preserve local scope, physical layout, Hina's POV and knowledge,
  ego-dystonic/non-causal intrusive thoughts, Calen's participation, prohibited
  content, and stop discipline. Reject unsupported durable change or generic
  fantasy escalation. One unchanged-prompt retry in a fresh context is allowed
  only if the first response is materially poor or malformed. If neither attempt
  can become acceptable without replacement-level rewriting, stop with
  `prose-quality-blocker`.
- Segment Reconciliation must compare accepted events with current canonical
  state, paraphrase minimal evidence, target valid visible record shapes, and
  propose only explicit durable deltas. Reject invented trust, recovery,
  bravery, diagnosis change, sword magic, threat identity, transfer cause, or
  any other inference not established by the accepted segment.

For every deliberately populated Generation Brief field, search the visible
prompt inspector for the distinctive saved value and visible label. Record
`present`, `absent`, or `unclear`, prominence, response evidence, and one of
`used`, `partly used`, `not compiled`, `not observably used`, `contradicted`, or
`not assessable`. Do not treat validation-only controls, IDs, author metadata,
blank optional fields, or hidden assumptions as prompt content. A single
one-field counterfactual is allowed only if an important visibly compiled field
appears ignored and the comparison could change a product decision; it remains
diagnostic and never enters the app.

## Acceptance and post-acceptance gate

Before clicking Accept, verify the candidate visibly satisfies every condition:

- Hina starts conscious on wet shrine stone.
- Injured Calen is still bracing the failing barred door.
- Outside pressure remains present but unidentified.
- The ceremonial sword is dormant, mounted, and within two steps.
- Hina's intrusive thoughts remain ego-dystonic and non-causal, with none of the
  prohibited mental-health or minor-content shortcuts.
- Hina deliberately agrees to help and grips the still-mounted sword.
- The prose ends there: no activation, removal, breach, combat, identified
  threat, transfer explanation, prophecy, mind-reading, or destiny claim.
- Any required edits are ordinary author edits rather than replacement-level
  rewriting.

Accept exactly once. Then visit **Accepted Segments** and visibly verify the new
sequence (expected sequence 1 for this fresh project) and its endpoint. Without
that proof, do not claim completion. Inspect the durable-change reminder.

Because the accepted endpoint plausibly changes Hina's commitment and physical
state, Segment Reconciliation is naturally justified unless the visible state
makes the need disappear; record either invocation or the exact skip reason.
Cold-evaluate its exact visible prompt. Treat every proposal as advisory. Through
canonical UI only, the author may record the explicit agreement and Hina's hand
on the still-mounted dormant sword while preserving Calen at the failing door
and the outside pressure as unidentified. Do not make accepted prose itself
canon and do not record inferred durable changes. Finish chosen record/brief
updates, verify them visibly, and stop before another prose generation.

## Evidence and finding discipline

- Keep charter, action notes, expected-versus-actual observations, field
  influence, prompt fingerprints, raw assessments, burden, and diagnostics in
  the `/tmp` scratch/exchange paths until the first report validation.
- Separate visible fact from interpretation. Give findings stable `F###` IDs,
  one primary classification, product category, confidence, visible
  reproduction, expected versus actual, impact, workaround/burden, likely
  layer, desired visible outcome, limits, and only evidence tags actually
  earned.
- Use routine screenshots only under `/tmp`. Promote only tightly scoped,
  privacy-safe artifacts that prove an important strength or finding. Do not
  retain full prompts, responses, candidate or accepted prose, project files,
  traces, session files, logs, API-key-like values, or uncited artifacts.
- Preserve prompt fingerprints, verdicts, bounded counts, burden, and the
  shortest necessary excerpts. The final Evidence Index must name every
  retained artifact and purpose, or say `No retained evidence.`
- If a probable blocker appears, record visible state, intended action,
  expectation, and last success; capture safe evidence; inspect existing guard
  and failure streams; then allow one visible recovery, one safe reload, and one
  fresh guarded browser against the same app/project. Never restart the app,
  replace the project, weaken validation, or silently omit a recovered failure.
- Any provider-send click or blocked provider route is an immediate blocked run
  with `completion_reason: provider-request-attempt`; confirm the guard abort and
  blank credential, record endpoint without content, and do not retry.

## Report and terminal handling

Write the unique cumulative `reports/<report-stem>.md` allocated by the helper.
Use the complete schema-v2 frontmatter and all sixteen required sections in the
prescribed order, including the validator-owned finding, prompt, field,
assistance, and cumulative-ledger table headers. The report must stand alone and
retain the sealed story contract, condensed visible journey, assistance skip
reasons, raw-response assessments, field influence, candidate burden,
acceptance proof, post-acceptance author work, privacy-safe evidence, limitations,
and a continuation handoff without reproducing accepted prose or full payloads.

For a successful run, report `status: completed`,
`completion_reason: accepted-one-segment`, accepted sequence 1 as visibly
verified, one or two cold prose attempts, the actual assistance-attempt count,
the actual intervention level, an existing preserved `/tmp` project, browser and
ephemeral loopback metadata, and zero send clicks, provider attempts, and
provider blocks. If the journey cannot satisfy the gate after bounded recovery,
write the same complete report with `status: blocked`, a specific kebab-case
reason, and `Not reached — <specific blocker>` for unreached phases. Never
manufacture completion.

Before closeout, copy safety counts and app/browser metadata. Request shutdown
of the guarded browser first and isolated app second, wait for clean exits, and
record unexpected termination. Stop only run-owned processes. Remove plumbing,
routine/forbidden evidence, and uncited artifacts, then validate the report.
After the first passing validation, delete the exchange directory and scratchpad,
validate again, and compare final `git status --short` with the sealed baseline.
The only allowed repository delta is the new report and its cited evidence
directory. Preserve the separate `/tmp` story project for continuation; remove
the successful run root only after proving it is the specific disposable child
and not the project. If validation never passes, retain and return the exact run
root rather than moving private material into the repository.

The terminal result is therefore either one visibly verified accepted segment
plus deliberate canonical post-acceptance review, or a truthful specific blocked
report—never an inferred success.
