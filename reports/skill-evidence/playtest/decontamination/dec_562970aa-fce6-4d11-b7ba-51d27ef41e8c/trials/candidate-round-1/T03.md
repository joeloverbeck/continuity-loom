# T03 Operator Packet — The Winter Letter

This packet specifies a future `new_story` playtest. It does not constitute a
completed run: no app, browser, project, prompt executor, or provider is to be
started while producing this packet. The real run ends after exactly one newly
accepted local segment and deliberate post-acceptance continuity work, or with
an honest terminal blocker after bounded recovery.

## Run charter

- Working title: **The Winter Letter**.
- Author stance: first-time, source-and-doc-blind author. Learn the product only
  from the visible 1440×900 browser UI, field help, and the author's own saved
  material. Do not inspect source, tests, docs, APIs, project files, SQLite,
  hidden state, or runtime internals during the journey.
- Intended local segment: on Christmas Eve 1972, Tomás Vidal enters Clara
  Hale's Wisconsin sanatorium room, presents bounded evidence, and earns no
  more than a reversible postponement of her intended suicide.
- Exact stop: after the bounded evidence, stop when Clara asks for proof or
  chooses another reversible step. Do not continue to discharge, a hospital
  scene, the woods, a completed rescue, or a second prose segment.
- Unresolved after the segment: whether Clara ultimately survives; what further
  proof Tomás can offer; any waking date or outcome for Daniel; discharge; and
  any later use of the woods route.
- POV and voice: close-third past through Tomás. He is a Spanish man born in
  1985, the sole time traveler, and roughly two thousand subjective years old.
  Convey that age through patience and judgment, not biographical exposition.
- Participation: Tomás is the POV and speaker; Clara is onstage, active, and
  fully agentic. Daniel remains offstage in 1972: in 2006 he asked Tomás to
  prevent his younger sister's suicide, but he must not appear in the room.
  Staff are included only in the visible function their actual physical
  presence requires; do not silently place them in the scene.
- Clara boundary: she is mathematically gifted, socially isolated, and believes
  Daniel's coma is permanent. Preserve her precision and agency without giving
  her diagnostic certainty or turning her into a savant caricature.
- Physical and continuity bounds: preserve the half-written letter, the room,
  corridor, staff, snow, and the established later woods-route geometry. Do not
  invent exact geometry where none has yet been authored; record it as a
  constraint or unresolved detail instead.
- Reveal and knowledge bounds: Clara may receive only the bounded evidence the
  author deliberately establishes. Do not reveal a waking date, use
  future-technology spectacle, or let her know offstage facts without an
  authored delivery path.
- Content and agency bounds: no coercion, romance, wealth pitch, completed
  rescue, or irreversible commitment. Her postponement must remain her choice.
- Expected canonical preparation: the story contract; focused cast and
  location/state records; explicit knowledge and reveal limits; the active
  working set; and a Generation Brief that names the local directive, physical
  state, voice, content boundaries, and stop condition. Private Notes are
  optional inert scratch only.
- Expected assistance: Ideate is natural only if the author genuinely needs
  historically grounded, bounded evidence possibilities before choosing one.
  Record Hygiene is natural only if the created records plausibly overlap or
  become stale. Segment Reconciliation is expected after acceptance if Clara's
  decision, the letter, locations, or another durable state actually changed.
  A skipped surface must receive its sincere naturalistic reason.
- Explicit non-goals: exhaustive feature coverage, optional-field completion,
  provider generation, diagnostic certainty, spectacle, a full life history,
  and resolution beyond the one reversible beat.

Seal this charter in the run scratchpad before the app starts. Later UI learning
may be recorded as confirmation, correction, or ambiguity, but must not rewrite
these expectations after the fact.

## Visible author journey

1. **Initial orientation.** After the witness has been assigned, have the main
   author record the screen's apparent purpose, next action, expected result,
   and unclear terms before systematic exploration. Keep witness and author
   accounts separate until both are fixed.
2. **Create visibly.** Use the visible **Create** form with the prepared parent
   path, folder, title, and a short premise description. Confirm the displayed
   project path and status. If the UI cannot create the prepared `/tmp` project,
   follow bounded recovery; do not create it through the filesystem or API.
3. **Story Configuration.** Express the one-segment contract, close-third past,
   maturity/content envelope, the agency and diagnostic boundaries, excluded
   outcomes, and the exact stop.
4. **Records.** Create only records required for this beat: focused dossiers for
   Tomás and Clara; Daniel as offstage pressure/context; the sanatorium room and
   only the corridor/staff/snow/woods-route facts needed to preserve physical
   geometry; the half-written letter; the coma belief and reveal boundary; and
   any bounded evidence the author actually chooses. Prefer atomic facts except
   where the UI presents a cast dossier as the natural shape. Do not manufacture
   a waking date, diagnosis, or future-tech mechanism.
5. **Private Notes.** Use them only for a sincere unsettled thought. Any adopted
   choice—especially the form of evidence—must be re-authored independently in
   a canonical record or the Generation Brief.
6. **Active Working Set.** Select only what this segment needs. Put Tomás in the
   POV function, Clara in the speaking/onstage function, any actually present
   silent participant in the corresponding visible function, and Daniel only in
   the offstage-pressure function. Do not promote staff into onstage presence
   merely for coverage.
7. **Generation Brief.** Fill the universal minimum plus only naturally useful
   context-gated fields. Include the entry into Clara's room, bounded evidence,
   Clara's reversible agency, Tomás's patient voice, physical anchors, knowledge
   limits, exclusions, and exact stop. Before compilation, write one influence
   row per deliberately populated prompt-facing field with its author need and
   intended observable effect. Do not count blank optional fields,
   validation-only controls, IDs, or author-only metadata.
8. **Readiness.** Save, distinguish warnings from blockers, and cure blockers
   only through canonical visible controls. Never weaken or bypass validation.
   Record any imposed work whose purpose remains unclear.
9. Make light accessibility/layout observations at safe points: focus
   visibility, accessible names, label/help association, non-color state,
   clipping, scrolling, long-form readability, and the 1440×900 layout. This is
   not a comprehensive accessibility audit.

## Cold prompt evaluations

For the prose prompt and each naturally invoked assistance prompt:

1. Before opening its inspector, record the specific author need and useful
   expected result. Once visibly open, record the visible prompt kind,
   fingerprint, versions, scope, counts, readiness, and source disclosure.
2. Use the supplied visible `text-file` action to copy the complete exact prompt
   into the `/tmp` exchange directory without printing it. Never obtain a hidden
   or unopened prompt.
3. Dispatch one genuinely fresh no-parent-context executor with only this
   instruction: `Read exactly the complete prompt in <absolute-prompt-file> and
   perform it as the sole task. Do not inspect the repository, any other file,
   the parent conversation, or prior responses. Return only the output requested
   by that prompt.` If file custody is required, add only the supplied
   response-file instruction. One fresh executor serves one attempt.
4. Never add this charter, the premise, desired answer, suspected defect, prior
   output, repository context, or evaluator expectations. Never inject the
   response into a provider-result UI. If fresh isolation is unavailable, stop
   with `cold-subagent-unavailable`.
5. Assess each untouched output before editing: contract compliance, grounding,
   unsupported invention, relevance, actionability, omissions, overreach,
   contradictions, false positives, adoption/rejection, repair burden, likely
   layer, verdict, and bounded confidence. For prose, specifically test the
   local room-only scope; close-third Tomás POV; patience-not-exposition voice;
   Clara's precision and agency; Daniel's offstage status; the coma/reveal
   boundary; half-written letter and physical geometry; excluded content; the
   reversible stop; and unsupported durable change.
6. A second prose attempt is allowed only if the first is materially poor or
   malformed. It must use the unchanged exact prompt and a new cold executor
   without critique. Choose the response an author would prefer to edit. If two
   attempts both require replacement-level rewriting, terminate with
   `prose-quality-blocker`.
7. For each populated Generation Brief field, search the visible inspector for
   its distinctive saved value and label. Record `present`, `absent`, or
   `unclear`, placement/prominence, response evidence, and one allowed influence
   verdict. Use at most one one-field counterfactual, only if an important
   visibly compiled field appears ignored and the result could affect a product
   decision; it stays diagnostic and never enters the app.
8. Evaluate Ideate for distinct, grounded, historically plausible, locally
   usable non-prose options or questions. Evaluate Record Hygiene for genuine
   changes versus keep-distinct reassurance, missed overlap, and unsafe merges.
   Evaluate Segment Reconciliation for real durable deltas against current
   state, valid targets/shapes, no invented canon, and clear manual work. If a
   surface was not sincerely needed, record the skip instead of manufacturing
   coverage.

The cold path does not exercise provider response parsing or provider result
cards, and the report must say so.

## Acceptance gate and post-acceptance continuity

1. Preserve the raw prose assessment before editing. Click **Write or paste
   candidate**, fill from the temporary preferred-response file, and make only
   ordinary visible author edits. Record burden as `none`, `light`,
   `substantial`, or `rewrite`.
2. Accept exactly once only if the edited candidate is coherent and satisfies
   the charter. Acceptance is not established by a toast or assumption: visit
   **Accepted Segments** and visibly verify the new positive sequence and its
   endpoint. Failure to reach or prove this state after bounded recovery is a
   blocker.
3. Inspect the durable-change reminder. Treat the accepted prose as evidence,
   never canon. Manually update, through visible canonical editors, only the
   durable facts the author chooses to adopt: for example, Clara's precisely
   bounded postponement, the current status of the letter, Tomás's location,
   and the exact evidence actually established. Preserve uncertainty, Daniel's
   offstage status, and every excluded outcome.
4. If Segment Reconciliation is naturally invoked, cold-evaluate it first and
   re-author only useful, correct proposals into records or brief fields. Do not
   claim reconciliation until those canonical surfaces visibly contain the
   chosen changes.
5. End the author journey after this post-acceptance work. Do not generate,
   paste, or accept a second segment.

## Blockers and bounded recovery

For a probable blocker, first freeze and record visible state, intended action,
expectation, and last success; capture privacy-safe evidence; then inspect only
existing guard/console/network summaries and, after documenting the visible
issue, a relevant accessibility tree, geometry, or visible DOM fragment. Try
one safe visible-control recovery, reload once only if persistence should be
safe, and finally make one fresh guarded-browser attempt against the same app
and project. Never restart the app, replace the project, randomly click, weaken
validation, or use direct state mutation. On recurrence or unsafe ambiguity,
stop and report the blocker.

Any send-control click or provider-block event ends the run immediately with
`status: blocked` and `completion_reason: provider-request-attempt`, even if the
guard blocked the request. Confirm the guard abort and blank credential, record
the visible action and endpoint without content, and never retry. Other terminal
conditions include launch failure, missing visible path to required state,
repeated loss/crash/stall, unavailable cold executor, two unusable prose
attempts, or unprovable acceptance. A blocked run still gets the complete report
shape and preserves its project.
