# T04 Operator Packet — Continuation Specificity Test

## Trial boundary

This is an instruction-level simulation of a future playtest run. Producing this packet must not
launch Continuity Loom, open or mutate the supplied project, inspect product source or product
documentation, drive a browser, create a real playtest report/evidence directory, or make a
provider request. The steps below are the run contract an operator would execute later under the
frozen baseline playtest skill.

## Run charter

- **Mode:** `continuation`.
- **Sole prior report:** `reports/playtest-the-winter-letter-2026-07-19T022000Z.md`. In an actual
  run, read that report completely and no unrelated report. Its frontmatter `project_path` is the
  only permitted project locator. It must resolve to an existing path under `/tmp`; otherwise
  produce a blocked report with `completion_reason: continuation-project-missing` rather than
  reconstructing the project from report prose.
- **Starting point:** the supplied task says accepted segment 1 ended with Clara postponing action
  until the next staff round and asking Tomás for three Daniel-specific facts. Treat that as a
  continuation expectation until the visible Accepted Segments archive confirms it. If the
  archive does not confirm latest sequence `1` and a compatible stopping point, stop rather than
  silently inventing a bridge.
- **One-segment objective:** accept at most one new local prose segment. If sequence `1` is visibly
  confirmed, the only successful terminal sequence is `2`. Do not compile, draft, or accept a
  second segment in this invocation.
- **Local scene:** Clara's sanatorium room, Christmas Eve 1972. Clara and Tomás remain physically
  in the room. Daniel is physically offstage and comatose in 1972. The approaching staff round is
  offstage pressure, not permission to displace the conversation before the stop.
- **Required action:** Tomás writes exactly three Daniel-specific, author-chosen facts on his own
  paperwork using a pencil Clara lends him. Clara tests each fact's specificity and possible
  source path. Clara retains control of the letter, interpersonal distance, evidentiary judgment,
  and any outcome.
- **Epistemic boundary:** the three statements may create credible paths to Daniel. They do not
  prove their own truth, time travel, Daniel's eventual recovery, or a waking date. Preserve the
  distinction among what Tomás asserts, what Clara hears or can test, and what the story has
  actually established.
- **Stopping boundary:** end on another reversible, local choice before staff pressure displaces
  the exchange. Do not leave the room, start rescue logistics, resolve the coma, force Clara's
  belief, settle the letter's outcome, or extend her commitment beyond the next bounded test or
  decision.
- **Authority boundary:** accepted prose is evidence of what occurred in the segment, not automatic
  continuity authority. Any durable continuity decision must be re-authored deliberately in a
  canonical visible Record or Generation Brief field after acceptance.

### Sealed author choices before launch

After reading only the supplied report, the operator privately chooses the exact three facts and
records them in the `/tmp` scratchpad, not in the durable report. Each fact must pass all of these
checks before it is entered through the UI:

1. It is specific to Daniel rather than a generic biographical detail.
2. It has a plausible source path Clara could later test without leaving the room in this segment.
3. Tomás can credibly present it as something he knows or claims under the inherited continuity.
4. It does not entail time travel, recovery, a waking date, or Clara's belief.
5. Its planned representation keeps assertion, corroboration path, and verified truth distinct.

Do not ask Ideate or the prose evaluator to invent these three facts. They are author decisions.
The exact fact text, accepted prose, and inherited report prose remain temporary/private and never
enter the repository report.

### Presence and agency matrix

| Participant or pressure | Physical status | Segment function | Guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Clara | In the sanatorium room | POV and evidentiary gatekeeper | Keeps the letter and controls distance, testing, and commitment |
| Tomás | In the room | Speaker; writes three facts on his own paperwork | Uses Clara's lent pencil; does not compel belief or take control |
| Daniel | Offstage; comatose in 1972 | Referent and offstage pressure only | Never appears, wakes, communicates, or becomes proof of time travel |
| Staff round | Offstage and approaching | Time pressure | Does not arrive or displace the conversation before the reversible stop |

## Cold prompt evaluations

### Exact prose prompt

1. Before opening the prompt inspector, record the author need and expected response. With the
   exact prompt visible, record its kind, fingerprint, displayed compiler/template versions,
   scope, record/field counts, readiness, and source disclosure.
2. In the visible inspector, search for each distinctive populated Generation Brief value and
   relevant label. Record `present`, `absent`, or `unclear`, visible placement/prominence, and the
   expected influence without putting full field bodies in the scratchpad or report.
3. Extract the complete visibly open prompt with `text-file` into the run's `/tmp` exchange
   directory. Do not print it, read it from an API/project file, or retain it in `reports/`.
4. Give one genuinely fresh cold context only the exact prompt-file instruction prescribed by the
   baseline skill, with `fork_turns: "none"` or the host's equivalent and no parent turns. If file
   custody is required, add only the prescribed response-file delivery instruction. Do not add the
   prior report, story explanation, desired result, suspected defect, or evaluator expectations.
5. Assess the untouched response before editing it. Judge contract/structure, grounding, all
   presence and knowledge limits, Clara's agency, exact count and treatment of the three
   author-chosen facts, the paperwork/pencil action, local scene, staff pressure, reversible stop,
   unsupported durable change, voice, genericity, and prose quality. Explicitly reject any output
   that turns the facts into proof, brings Daniel onstage or awake, forces belief, begins logistics,
   leaves the room, or overruns the stopping boundary.
6. Record the universal usefulness verdict (`useful`, `mixed`, `low-value`, `misleading`,
   `malformed`, or `blocked`), adoption/rejection, likely layer, confidence, and expected editing
   burden. One stochastic result is suggestive, not causal proof.
7. If the first response is meaningfully poor or malformed, allow exactly one fresh retry using
   the unchanged prompt and no feedback about attempt one. Preserve both raw assessments and
   choose the response an author would rather edit. If neither can become acceptable without a
   replacement-level rewrite, stop with `completion_reason: prose-quality-blocker`.
8. A targeted counterfactual is allowed at most once only if one important populated field is
   visibly compiled yet appears ignored and the result could change a product decision. Change
   that field alone, fingerprint both prompts, use another fresh context, and keep the result
   diagnostic only—never paste it into the app or continuity state.

### Assistance prompts

For every naturally invoked assistance surface, predeclare the author need, inspect and fingerprint
the exact visible prompt, extract it under `/tmp`, and send it alone to a fresh cold context. Apply
the same universal verdict and at-most-one unchanged-prompt retry limit. Never click the provider
control and never inject a cold response into a provider-result card.

- **Ideate:** skip unless a genuine structural uncertainty arises after the author has independently
  fixed all three facts. Do not adopt system-invented Daniel facts.
- **Record Hygiene:** if naturally invoked, distinguish actionable changes from legitimate
  no-change findings and manually apply only safe, useful advice through visible Record editors.
- **Segment Reconciliation:** when naturally warranted after acceptance, judge whether it
  distinguishes Tomás's assertions, Clara's knowledge/testing options, and verified continuity;
  preserves paraphrase and valid targets; and avoids promoting accepted prose or the three
  statements into canon. Re-author only approved, correctly scoped durable changes.

No paired-draw check is performed because its authoritative state is `awaiting-disposition`.
Provider parsing and assistance-result cards remain explicitly untested on this cold path.

## Candidate and acceptance gate

1. Freeze the raw prose assessment first. Then use visible **Write or paste candidate** and
   `fill-file` to enter the selected temporary response. Make only ordinary author edits and log
   intervention as `none`, `light`, `substantial`, or `rewrite`; do not silently replace most of
   the output to hide a prompt failure.
2. Before acceptance, visibly verify every gate:
   - the segment stays in Clara's sanatorium room on Christmas Eve 1972;
   - Clara remains the controlling POV and retains the letter, distance, judgment, and outcome;
   - Tomás writes exactly three pre-authored Daniel-specific facts on his own paperwork using the
     lent pencil;
   - Clara tests specificity and possible source paths but is not forced to believe;
   - Daniel remains physically offstage and comatose in 1972;
   - no statement proves time travel, recovery, or a waking date;
   - staff remains approaching pressure rather than displacing the conversation;
   - the ending is one reversible local choice; and
   - there is no room departure, rescue logistics, coma resolution, settled letter outcome, or
     commitment beyond the local boundary.
3. Accept only coherent prose the author genuinely chooses. If the gate cannot be met within the
   two cold-attempt/rewrite boundary, block honestly. Never claim success from an editor state or
   button click alone.
4. After the single acceptance, revisit **Accepted Segments** and visibly verify that latest
   sequence advanced by exactly one (expected `1` to `2`). Capture the durable-change reminder.
   Do not generate again.
5. Review any durable changes through Segment Reconciliation and/or Record Hygiene only when
   naturally useful. Manually encode chosen continuity so that the durable state can say Tomás
   made particular assertions and Clara has particular source paths, without asserting that the
   propositions are verified. Leave the Generation Brief and working set in a deliberate next-run
   state and preserve every still-open choice.

## Observation and evidence contract

- Append a structured scratchpad observation for each meaningful strength, snag, mismatch,
  prompt verdict, retry, correction, edit burden, recovery, acceptance milestone, and
  post-acceptance decision. Keep `Observed fact` distinct from `Author interpretation`; never
  rewrite an earlier misunderstanding—append a correction.
- For a probable blocker, stop and capture the visible state and intended action first. Then inspect
  only existing privacy-safe console/network/guard streams and narrowly scoped visible diagnostics.
  Permit at most one visible recovery action, one reload, and one fresh guarded browser against the
  same app/project. If it recurs, write a blocked report.
- Routine screenshots, exact prompts, raw responses, candidate prose, accepted prose, settings,
  and scratch material stay under the unique `/tmp` run root. Retain under
  `reports/assets/<stem>/` only tightly scoped artifacts that prove a finding or important
  strength.
- Preferred retained proof for T04, when privacy-safe and actually needed, is: the visible
  continuation/sequence state without prose; a narrowly scoped prompt-inspector metadata or field
  presence state without prompt text; a candidate/acceptance control state without candidate
  prose; the post-acceptance sequence `2` without accepted prose; the durable-change reminder; and
  any finding-specific sanitized crop. Fingerprint promoted artifacts.
- Never retain or report full prompts, full record payloads, raw assistance, candidate or accepted
  prose, project files/database, API keys, app logs, traces, browser profiles, routine shots,
  session plumbing, or uncited evidence. Every retained artifact receives one privacy-safe purpose
  line in the report's Evidence Index.
