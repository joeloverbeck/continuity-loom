# T03 — New Story with Transfer Evidence: Operator Packet

## Run charter

- Mode: `new_story`.
- Working title: *The Winter Letter*.
- Boundary: create one isolated `/tmp` project, author and visibly accept exactly one local prose segment, complete deliberate post-acceptance continuity work, then stop.
- Author stance: source-and-doc blind. Learn the product only through its visible 1440×900 browser UI, field help, readiness messages, and prompt inspector.
- Story state:
  - Tomás Vidal: Spanish, born 1985, sole time traveler, roughly two thousand subjective years old.
  - Daniel Hale asks Tomás in 2006 to prevent Clara Hale’s 1972 suicide.
  - Clara is Daniel’s younger sister, mathematically gifted, socially isolated, and believes his coma is permanent.
  - Scene: Clara’s Wisconsin sanatorium room, Christmas Eve 1972.
- Segment intent: Tomás enters, offers a deliberately author-designed but bounded and falsifiable form of evidence, and earns at most a reversible postponement.
- Viewpoint and voice: close-third past through Tomás; convey age through patience and conduct, not biographical exposition.
- Agency and knowledge:
  - Clara remains precise, skeptical, and capable of choosing.
  - No diagnostic certainty or savant caricature.
  - Daniel remains offstage and cannot speak or appear.
  - Clara cannot know future outcomes that Tomás has not deliberately revealed.
- Physical continuity: preserve the half-written letter, room, corridor, staff, snow, and later woods-route geometry.
- Forbidden developments: coercion, romance, wealth pitch, future-technology spectacle, Daniel’s waking date, discharge, hospital scene, completed rescue, or any irreversible cure/resolution.
- Stop condition: end when Clara asks for further proof or chooses another reversible step. Leave the larger rescue, future disclosures, and woods-route outcome unresolved.

Before launch, seal these expectations, the intended record set, anticipated brief fields, expected assistance needs, and non-goals in the run scratchpad. Quantitative tracking remains inactive because the task requests no action-count comparison.

## Cold prompt evaluations

For prose and every naturally invoked assistance surface:

1. Record the author need and expected output before opening the prompt inspector.
2. Record visible kind, fingerprint, versions, scope, source counts, and readiness disclosure.
3. Search the visible prose prompt for each deliberately populated brief field and record `present`, `absent`, or `unclear`, plus placement and prominence.
4. Extract the complete visibly open prompt to the run’s `/tmp` exchange directory without printing it.
5. Send only that exact prompt to a fresh context with no parent turns, story briefing, expected answer, suspected defect, or prior response. Have the raw output saved verbatim under `/tmp`.
6. Assess the untouched response for:
   - output-contract compliance;
   - grounding and unsupported invention;
   - POV, knowledge, cast, voice, physical continuity, and stop discipline;
   - Clara’s agency and avoidance of caricature;
   - prohibited developments;
   - actionable value, omissions, overreach, and author intervention burden.
7. Give each response a `useful`, `mixed`, `low-value`, `misleading`, `malformed`, or `blocked` verdict and distinguish prompt-contract problems from model-execution failures.
8. Allow one unchanged-prompt prose retry only if the first response is meaningfully poor or malformed. The retry gets a separate fresh context and no critique of the first response.
9. Use no counterfactual unless one important, visibly compiled field appears ignored and a single-field diagnostic could materially clarify the issue. Counterfactual output is never eligible for the app.
10. Assistance suggestions are never injected into result cards. Adopted suggestions are manually re-authored through visible canonical editors.

No paired draw is manufactured. The task requires first-view and claim-challenge evidence, not an otherwise ineligible additional assistance pair.

## Acceptance gate

A candidate is acceptable only if it:

- begins within the specified Christmas Eve 1972 sanatorium-room moment;
- uses close-third past through Tomás;
- conveys his age through patience rather than exposition;
- preserves Clara’s precision, agency, uncertainty, and knowledge limits;
- keeps Daniel offstage;
- uses only bounded evidence;
- preserves the letter and room/corridor/staff/snow/woods-route geometry;
- avoids every forbidden escalation;
- ends at a request for proof or another reversible choice;
- does not silently create unsupported durable canon.

Before pasting, preserve the raw-response assessment. Use **Write or paste candidate**, load the selected temporary response through the visible editor, and make only ordinary author edits. Record intervention as `none`, `light`, `substantial`, or `rewrite`.

If neither allowed cold attempt can satisfy the charter without replacement-level rewriting, stop with `status: blocked` and `completion_reason: prose-quality-blocker`. Do not fabricate acceptance.

Otherwise:

1. Accept exactly one coherent segment.
2. Visit Accepted Segments and visibly verify sequence `1`.
3. Inspect the durable-change reminder.
4. Evaluate Segment Reconciliation if the accepted event plausibly changes durable state.
5. Manually update records or the Generation Brief for only the continuity the author deliberately adopts.
6. Preserve the accepted prose as evidence, not canon.
7. Leave the project ready for continuation, then stop without generating a second segment.

- Put all selected claims into one privacy-safe packet.

- Send the packet to one fresh context and require one rival explanation and one observable discriminator per claim.

- The main operator resolves the final wording and adds the matching evidence tag. Independent advice never auto-deletes a finding or validates product semantics.
