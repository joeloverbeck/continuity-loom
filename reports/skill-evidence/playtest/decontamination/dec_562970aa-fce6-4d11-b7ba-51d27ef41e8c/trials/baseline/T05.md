# T05 Baseline Operator Packet — Continuation Author-Invention Boundary

## Packet status

This is an instruction-level simulation for T05. It specifies the run but does not execute it: do
not launch Continuity Loom, open or mutate the historical project, drive a browser, dispatch cold
evaluators, or make any provider request while producing this packet. A later real run would use
the guarded workflow below.

## Run charter

- Mode: `continuation`.
- Sole prior report: `reports/playtest-the-winter-letter-2026-07-20T023325Z.md`. Read that report
  completely in an actual run and no other earlier playtest report. Obtain the project only from
  its `project_path`; require that path to be an existing child of `/tmp`. A missing, non-`/tmp`,
  or unopenable path ends in a truthful blocked report rather than reconstruction from prose.
- Author stance: source-and-doc blind throughout the product journey. Learn the product through
  visible UI and field help only. Do not inspect source, tests, app docs, APIs, SQLite, project
  files, hidden state, or the live DOM to advance the story.
- Segment boundary: accept exactly one new local segment after the prior latest segment. Segment 2
  is expected from the task, but confirm the actual latest sequence in **Accepted Segments** before
  changing state. The new sequence is the visibly confirmed prior sequence plus one (expected 3),
  never an assumed number.
- Local scene: Clara's sanatorium room, Christmas Eve 1972.
- Required author decision: Clara's test is unresolved on entry. During this run, the author must
  design and choose a test that she can set up and check herself with ordinary 1972 objects and
  that does not depend on Tomás's testimony, Daniel's testimony, or Clara wanting either account
  to be true. Assistance may offer alternatives or questions, but neither an assistance response
  nor prose may make this fact authoritative. Before compiling prose, the author must deliberately
  choose the test and re-author its terms through the visible canonical Records and/or Generation
  Brief surface appropriate to the UI.
- Required stopping state: the test terms are fixed and Clara has begun sealing her question. The
  test result and the sealed question remain unknown. Clara's commitment remains bounded at
  breakfast.
- Prohibited developments: do not deliver the result, reveal the sealed question, leave the room,
  wake Daniel, extend Clara's commitment beyond breakfast, establish time travel as proven, or
  give staff a speaking part.
- Completion: one coherent segment is visibly accepted and post-acceptance continuity is
  deliberately reconciled, or the bounded blocker policy is exhausted. Never manufacture
  completion.

## Visible returning-author journey

### Re-establish the continuation point

1. Open the prior report's exact project path through the visible **Open** form; confirm displayed
   title and path.
2. Visit **Accepted Segments** before editing. Establish the latest accepted sequence, confirm that
   Segment 2 is the current endpoint or record the discrepancy, and assess whether the archive
   makes the continuation point understandable.
3. Read accepted prose only as returning-author review. Do not copy it into prompt-facing fields,
   treat it as canon, or derive automatic durable state from it. Review the visible durable-change
   reminder and identify any deliberately unfinished reconciliation inherited from the prior run.
4. Append contemporaneous scratchpad observations that separate visible fact from author
   interpretation. Preserve initial misunderstandings and append corrections instead of rewriting
   them.

### Make the missing test an author decision

1. Review Story Configuration, existing Records, Private Notes, Active Working Set, and the saved
   Generation Brief through the UI only. Confirm the continuing constraints without importing
   accepted prose as continuity authority.
2. Keep Private Notes inert. They may hold the author's questions or discarded options, but any
   adopted choice must be re-authored deliberately in a prompt-facing canonical surface.
3. Establish a visible decision checklist before choosing the test:
   - Clara can set it up and check it herself;
   - only ordinary objects plausibly available in 1972 are involved;
   - its evidentiary force does not depend on Tomás, Daniel, or motivated belief;
   - its outcome will occur after this segment and cannot prove time travel;
   - the question being sealed remains undisclosed;
   - nothing requires leaving the room, waking Daniel, broadening the breakfast commitment, or
     giving staff dialogue.
4. If the author sincerely wants alternatives or diagnostic questions, invoke **Ideate** through
   its visible compile/inspect path and cold-evaluate the exact exposed prompt as specified below.
   The output remains advice. The author independently rejects, combines, or chooses suggestions,
   and no suggestion enters authority by copy-through.
5. The author now makes the test decision. Re-author its chosen terms manually into the visible
   canonical Record or Generation Brief fields that the UI presents for the next local segment.
   Do not paste raw assistance output. Record the rationale and the exact author need in the
   scratchpad without copying a full story payload into retained evidence.
6. Treat this as a hard pre-prose checkpoint: do not compile the prose prompt until the visible
   saved canonical state contains the author-chosen test terms and the scratchpad records their
   expected influence. If the UI offers no reasonable canonical path, use bounded recovery and,
   if unresolved, end with an honest `ui-dead-end` or `readiness-dead-end` report rather than let
   generated prose decide the fact.

### Prepare the local segment

1. Curate only records needed for this segment. Put each intended POV, speaker, silent/onstage
   presence, physically active character, present-minor participant, and offstage pressure in the
   appropriate visible working-set/cast function. Staff must not be assigned a speaking function.
2. Fill the universal Generation Brief minimum and only naturally relevant gated fields. Ensure
   the manual directive asks for the already-chosen test terms to be fixed, Clara to begin sealing
   her unrevealed question, and the segment to stop before any result.
3. For every deliberately populated prompt-facing field, write a pre-compilation ledger row with
   its author need and intended observable influence. Include the room/date, knowledge and reveal
   locks, testimony-independence, ordinary-object/self-check constraints, no-result stop, hidden
   question, breakfast-bounded commitment, staff-silence, and any cast-presence controls wherever
   those needs are represented. Do not assume a field is useful merely because it exists.
4. Save the draft, inspect visible readiness blockers and warnings, and resolve only through the
   canonical UI. Never weaken or bypass validation.

## Cold prompt evaluations

### Common protocol

For every naturally invoked prompt, record the author need and expected result before opening its
inspector. With the exact prompt visibly open, record its displayed kind, fingerprint,
template/compiler versions when shown, scope, counts, readiness, and source disclosures. Use the
inspector search to check each deliberately populated field's distinctive saved value and label;
record `present`, `absent`, or `unclear`, its visible placement, and its prominence without copying
the full value.

Extract only the complete visibly displayed prompt with `text-file` into the run's temporary
exchange directory. Send that exact file to one genuinely fresh context with no inherited turns,
story brief, desired answer, prior report, suspected defect, repository context, or prior response.
The evaluator receives only the baseline's fixed instruction to perform the prompt as its sole task
and, when needed, save the verbatim response to a fresh exchange file. Never use OpenRouter and
never click Generate, Ideate, Analyze, Review, model refresh, or another provider-send control.

For every untouched response, assess structure, grounding, unsupported invention, local relevance,
actionable value, omissions, contradictions, author adoption/rejection, transformation burden,
likely layer, and one of `useful`, `mixed`, `low-value`, `misleading`, `malformed`, or `blocked`.
Keep prompt-contract mismatch separate from model-output failure. Exact prompts and raw responses
stay under `/tmp` and never enter the report.

### Ideate, if naturally invoked

Evaluate whether the output supplies distinct, grounded, non-prose alternatives or questions that
respect the decision checklist. Reject anything that supplies a result, reveals the sealed
question, relies on testimony or belief, proves time travel, uses anachronistic/unavailable objects,
leaves the room, wakes Daniel, extends commitment, or gives staff speech. Count only options the
author seriously considered. Even a useful option is not authority: adoption occurs only through
the author's separate choice and manual canonical entry before prose compilation.

### Prose prompt

Dispatch one fresh cold evaluator for the exact visible prose prompt. Allow one fresh retry with
the unchanged bytes only if the first response is meaningfully poor or malformed; do not tell the
second evaluator what failed. Assess each raw response separately. Judge whether it follows the
already-authored test, remains in Clara's room and POV, respects cast/knowledge/voice constraints,
ends after sealing begins, and withholds the result, question, time-travel proof, wider commitment,
Daniel waking, departure, and staff speech. Generated prose may realize the author-chosen terms but
may not originate or silently alter them.

Choose the response an author would genuinely rather edit. If neither allowed attempt can be made
acceptable without replacement-level rewriting, stop with `prose-quality-blocker`. Do not create a
third draw or fabricate a successful candidate.

### Field influence and optional diagnostics

After response review, complete every Generation Brief field row as `used`, `partly used`, `not
compiled`, `not observably used`, `contradicted`, or `not assessable`, with evidence and confidence.
Validation-only or author-only controls are listed separately rather than mislabeled as ignored
prose context. At most one targeted counterfactual may be used only if an important field was
visibly compiled but appears ignored and the comparison could change a product decision. Change
that one field only, use one new cold context, label the result suggestive, and never use its prose
in the app or continuation state.

Record Hygiene and Segment Reconciliation are cold-evaluated by the same exact-visible-prompt
protocol only if naturally useful. For reconciliation, reject any proposal that treats prose as
automatic canon, invents the result or sealed question, overstates the test as proof, or broadens
Clara's commitment. Because the Paired-Draw pilot is awaiting disposition, use one normal cold
response per naturally invoked assistance prompt (plus the baseline's ordinary retry rule when
applicable), never a pilot pair.

## Candidate and acceptance gate

1. Preserve the selected raw-prose assessment before entry. Open **Write or paste candidate** and
   use `fill-file` with the temporary chosen response; never inject it into the DOM or project.
2. Visibly edit as an ordinary author. Remove or repair any invented test variation, result,
   revealed question, proof claim, location/time violation, awakened Daniel, extended commitment,
   or staff speech. Do not silently rewrite most of the response to hide prompt failure.
3. Record intervention as `none`, `light`, `substantial`, or `rewrite`. A replacement-level rewrite
   is not an acceptable success path under this run's cold-prose gate.
4. Before accepting, visibly confirm all of the following:
   - the prose instantiates the author-chosen canonical test and does not alter it;
   - Clara can set up/check it herself with ordinary 1972 objects;
   - the terms do not depend on either man's testimony or Clara's desired belief;
   - no result appears and the test does not prove time travel;
   - the scene remains in Clara's sanatorium room on Christmas Eve 1972;
   - Clara begins sealing the question, but its content is not revealed;
   - her commitment remains bounded at breakfast;
   - Daniel does not wake, no one leaves, and staff do not speak;
   - the segment stops at the specified unresolved point.
5. Accept exactly once through the visible UI. Visit **Accepted Segments** and verify the new latest
   sequence and readable archive entry. Acceptance is not proof that any prose statement is canon.
6. Inspect the durable-change reminder. If the segment creates durable consequences that the author
   deliberately wants, use Segment Reconciliation and/or Record Hygiene when naturally useful,
   cold-evaluate them, and manually re-author only independently accepted changes into canonical
   Records or Generation Brief. Preserve the unknown result and question and the breakfast limit.
7. Leave Records, Generation Brief, and working set in a deliberate state for the next run. Stop;
   do not begin a second prose generation.

## Observation, evidence, and blocker handling

- Append a structured observation for each meaningful first occurrence, repeat, expectation
  mismatch, strength, friction, confusion, defect, prompt verdict, retry, edit burden, recovery,
  accepted-segment milestone, and post-acceptance decision. Keep observed facts distinct from
  interpretations and record corrections without deleting superseded observations.
- After consequential visible actions, retain routine screenshots only under `/tmp`. Promote only
  tightly scoped, privacy-safe evidence that supports a finding or important strength. Never retain
  full prompts, record payloads, raw assistance, candidate prose, accepted prose, project files,
  secrets, browser traces, or screenshots exposing those bodies.
- A probable blocker permits, in order: record the visible state; capture safe evidence; inspect
  existing diagnostic streams and only then relevant visible-tree/geometry/DOM fragments; try one
  safe visible recovery; reload once if state should persist; and make one fresh guarded-browser
  attempt against the same app/project. Do not restart the app, replace the project, repeatedly
  click, bypass validation, or switch to APIs/files.
- Any provider control click or guard event ends the run immediately with blocked status and
  `provider-request-attempt`, after confirming the guard and blank credential. A missing project,
  unavailable cold context, unsafe state, unrecoverable UI path, or unacceptable prose after two
  attempts similarly ends in the matching honest blocked report.
