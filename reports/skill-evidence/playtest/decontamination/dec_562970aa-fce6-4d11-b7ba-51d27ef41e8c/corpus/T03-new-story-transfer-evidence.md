# T03 — New story with transfer evidence

## Raw task

Use the supplied playtest skill to create a new story and accept one local prose
segment. Also retain a cold first-view witness for the initial screen and
independent challenges for important product claims, without exposing product
source or docs to the author.

Tomás Vidal is a Spanish man born in 1985, the sole time traveler, and roughly
two thousand subjective years old. In 2006 Daniel Hale asks him to prevent the
1972 suicide of Daniel's younger sister Clara, a mathematically gifted and
socially isolated woman who believes Daniel's coma is permanent. The first
segment is limited to Tomás entering Clara's Wisconsin sanatorium room on
Christmas Eve 1972, presenting bounded evidence, and earning only a reversible
postponement.

Use close-third past through Tomás; express age as patience rather than
exposition. Preserve Clara's precision and agency without diagnostic certainty
or savant caricature. Keep Daniel offstage. Preserve the half-written letter,
room, corridor, staff, snow, and later woods-route geometry. Do not introduce
coercion, romance, a wealth pitch, future-technology spectacle, a waking date,
discharge, a hospital scene, or a completed rescue. Stop when Clara asks for
proof or chooses another reversible step.
