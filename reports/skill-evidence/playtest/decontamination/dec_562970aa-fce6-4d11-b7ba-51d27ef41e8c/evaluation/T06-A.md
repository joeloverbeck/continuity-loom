## Run charter

- **Mode:** `new_story`; no prior report or historical project is in scope.
- **Working title:** *The Noon Lockout*.
- **POV and presence:** Nera Vale is the sole physically present character and the sole
  close-third viewpoint. There are no other onstage people, speakers, or silent presences.
- **Offstage pressure:** Iven Marr exists only as the issuer named on an already-stamped paper
  launch order. Represent him minimally and ENTITY-first. Put him in an offstage-pressure cast
  function only if the visible working-set/readiness UI requires that selection. Never promote him
  to an onstage, speaking, or fully specified character to satisfy a form.
- **Local segment:** Nera discovers and verifies a hairline fracture in the unmanned tidal
  ferry's drive coupling; refuses the unsafe noon launch; photographs and tags the defect; locks
  out the drive; and preserves the evidence.
- **Hard stop:** stop before Iven appears. His possible arrival and confrontation belong no
  earlier than the following segment and remain unaccepted future intent, not present canon.
- **Forbidden additions:** no live Iven speech, location, current action, thoughts, or intentions;
  no third person, crowd, call, radio voice, flashback speaker, injury, passenger peril, romance,
  or violence.
- **Durable-authority boundary:** accepted prose is readable evidence of what was accepted, but it
  does not automatically update records, working-set authority, or a future Generation Brief.
  Every durable change the author chooses to keep must be re-authored explicitly through the
  visible canonical surfaces after acceptance.
- **Non-goals:** do not enlarge Iven's biography, appearance, relationships, motivations, or voice;
  do not add a private note merely to exercise the feature; do not invent additional launch-test
  stakeholders or hazards.

Before launch, seal this charter, the expected author workflow, and the expectation that the app
can express one onstage POV plus a pressure-only offstage entity without demanding character
inflation. Preserve later corrections as corrections rather than rewriting the initial model.
