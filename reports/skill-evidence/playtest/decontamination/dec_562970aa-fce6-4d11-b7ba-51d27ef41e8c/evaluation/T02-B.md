This packet is an instruction-level run plan, not a claim that the run occurred.
The paired trial must not launch the app, touch the historical project, execute a
prompt, or create any artifact other than this packet. A later operator executing
the run must follow the guarded workflow below.

  prose segment.

  `reports/playtest-the-unbidden-oath-2026-07-17T104952Z.md`.

## Sealed pre-use charter

Seal this charter in the allocated scratchpad before app launch. Do not revise
it in light of later product documentation.

### Story and local unit

Hina Mori, in close-third past, continues immediately after sequence 1. Her
primarily obsessional OCD remains ego-dystonic distress expressed through
intrusive thoughts and covert checking. It is never magic, intent, prophecy,
violence propensity, a power, or something cured by transport, bravery, or
combat.

The local unit begins immediately after Hina grips the shrine sword. Calen has
asked her to cover him, not replace him at the crossbar. The segment must:

1. have Hina draw the still-dormant, ordinary sword;
2. move her behind and inward of injured Calen while he remains responsible for
   the crossbar;
3. render one sustained impact that does not breach the door;
4. let the outside scraping stop and give Hina and Calen several shared,
   listening breaths; and
5. end on Calen warning Hina not to lower the sword.

The exact stop is the warning itself, before Hina responds. Do not open the
door, identify the outside threat, begin combat, activate or magicalize the
sword, explain Veyr or the transfer, resolve Hina's uncertainty, or imply that
her intrusive thoughts reveal danger.

### Presence, knowledge, physical state, and voice

- POV: Hina; close-third past; free-indirect access remains bounded to what she
  can perceive and know.
- Onstage physical participants: Hina and Calen only. Both may speak; Calen must
  deliver the terminal warning. There is no silent third onstage presence.
- Offstage pressure: the unidentified force outside the barred door. Its impact
  and scraping may be perceived, but its identity, nature, number, intent, and
  capabilities remain unknown.
- Hina begins wet, frightened, untrained, and physically uninjured, with one
  hand on a mounted sword. Drawing and moving do not grant competence.
- Calen remains injured at the crossbar. Hina covers him from behind and inward;
  she does not supplant him or erase his injury.
- The shrine door remains closed and unbreached. The crossbar, sword custody,
  room geometry, and pressure state may change only as explicitly rendered.
- Voice: restrained, immediate, sensory, and non-diagnostic. Covert checking may
  recur, but observable facts and chosen limited action—not certainty or cure—
  return Hina to the moment.

### Authoring and assistance expectations

Reuse the existing configuration, records, continuation validation context, and
working set when visibly appropriate. Expected useful authority includes the
Hina and Calen entity/cast records, Hina's private belief, the shrine location,
the dormant sword object, the door pressure clock, and only other existing facts
needed for this unit. Do not create records merely for coverage.

There is a sincere pre-commitment need for grounded options about how the
outside pressure changes while remaining unidentified, so **Ideate** may be used
before the directive is finalized. Adopt nothing automatically. Private Notes
have no planned use; skip them unless genuine inert scratch becomes necessary,
and record the naturalistic skip. **Segment Reconciliation** is expected after
acceptance. **Record Hygiene** becomes appropriate only after the explicit
CLOCK, LOCATION, and OBJECT updates have been manually authored at the natural
post-acceptance point.

The first-time-returning-author model is: accepted prose is inspectable evidence
of what was rendered, while Records and the Generation Brief remain the only
continuity authority. Non-goals include provider generation, exhaustive
optional-field completion, unrelated retesting, a second segment, and any
attempt to repair the product.
