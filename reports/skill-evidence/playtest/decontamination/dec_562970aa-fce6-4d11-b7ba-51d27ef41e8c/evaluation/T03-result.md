# T03 Paired Evaluation Result

## Decision

**A wins.** A satisfies every shared and task-specific criterion and gives the two requested transfer-evidence instruments valid, auditable procedures. B is shorter and generally safe, but materially weakens both the sealed first-view witness and the independent-claim challenge requirement.

## Regression classification

- **A:** no material regression; no severe regression.
- **B:** material regression; no severe regression. The regression is material because the task specifically requires uncontaminated first-view evidence and independent challenges for important product claims. B does not reliably produce either instrument as specified. It does not authorize a provider request, manufacture acceptance, canonize accepted prose, mutate a supplied historical project, or omit the cumulative report, so the failure is not severe under the frozen rubric.

## Burden and tradeoff

A is substantially longer and imposes more operator bookkeeping, especially for first-view comparison, two separately isolated challengers, blocker recovery, report schema, and cleanup. That burden is justified here because T03 is explicitly a transfer-evidence trial and those details prevent contamination, false independence, overclaiming, and evidence leakage. B is easier to scan and execute at a high level, but its brevity removes the guarantees that distinguish this task from an ordinary new-story playtest.

## Bottom line

A is the only candidate that reliably yields one uncontaminated first-view observation, two independently challenged important product claims including a critical gate, and a cumulative evidence-backed report without weakening provider, continuity-authority, acceptance, or custody boundaries.
