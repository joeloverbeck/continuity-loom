# T03 Operator Packet — The Winter Letter

## Run charter

This is a `new_story` author playtest. The operator is source-and-doc blind for
the author journey and learns the product only from the visible 1440x900 browser
UI and its field help. The run may create one fresh project under the exact
`/tmp` path allocated by the run-preparation receipt, accept exactly one local
prose segment, perform deliberate post-acceptance continuity work, and then
stop. It must not use product source, tests, docs, APIs, SQLite, project files,
hidden state, DOM mutation, runtime evaluation, or any pre-existing app process.

Working title: **The Winter Letter**.

Sealed story intent:

- Tomás Vidal is a Spanish man born in 1985, the sole time traveler, and about
  two thousand subjective years old. His age appears as patience, restraint,
  and practiced attention, not biography or explanatory monologue.
- In 2006 Daniel Hale asks Tomás to prevent the 1972 suicide of Daniel's younger
  sister Clara. In 1972 Clara believes Daniel's coma is permanent. Daniel
  remains offstage throughout this segment.
- The segment begins with Tomás entering Clara's Wisconsin sanatorium room on
  Christmas Eve 1972. Clara is mathematically gifted, precise, socially
  isolated, and fully agentic; neither the records nor prose may assert a
  diagnosis or turn her into a savant caricature.
- Tomás presents one bounded, non-coercive piece of evidence: a private family
  recollection supplied by Daniel whose significance Clara can recognize but
  whose provenance she cannot yet prove. The operator must author the exact
  recollection as a canonical fact before compilation. It may make Tomás worth
  questioning; it must not force trust, settle time travel, or guarantee rescue.
- Exact stop: Clara either asks Tomás for proof or chooses one other reversible
  next step. The result is only a postponement. Her ultimate decision, the
  provenance of Tomás's knowledge, Daniel's waking date, any discharge, and any
  completed rescue remain unresolved.
- Close-third past through Tomás. Clara and Tomás may speak. There are no other
  onstage people or silent onstage presences. Daniel and nearby staff are
  offstage pressures.
- Preserve the half-written letter in the room, the room-to-corridor relation,
  staff nearby in the corridor, snow outside, and the established possibility
  of a later route toward the woods. No person or object may move in a way that
  breaks that geometry.
- Do not introduce coercion, romance, a wealth pitch, future-technology
  spectacle, a waking date, discharge, a hospital scene, or a completed rescue.
  Do not let Tomás know or reveal more about Clara's private state than the
  authored family evidence and observable room allow.

The expected useful canonical material is deliberately narrow: story contract
and prose/maturity constraints; dossiers for Tomás, Clara, and offstage Daniel;
the family-evidence fact; Clara's belief and reveal boundary; Tomás's knowledge
and limits; the half-written letter; the Wisconsin room/corridor/staff/snow and
later woods-route geometry; and the immediate pressure and reversible stop.
The Active Working Set should include Tomás as POV/speaker, Clara as
speaker/present participant, Daniel only as offstage pressure, and only the
records needed for this moment. No record is created merely to exercise a UI.

Private Notes are allowed only if the author sincerely needs inert scratch—for
example, comparing two formulations of the family recollection or two
reversible postponements. Any adopted choice must be separately authored into a
canonical record or visible Generation Brief field. Ideate is expected only if
the author genuinely lacks a grounded reversible next step. Record Hygiene is
expected only if the created facts plausibly overlap or become stale. Segment
Reconciliation is expected after acceptance if the segment changes Clara's
posture, the letter's state, or another durable fact. Every skipped assistance
surface gets a naturalistic reason in the final report.

Before launch, write this charter and the following first-time-author model to
the allocated scratchpad: the product appears to turn deliberately selected
story records plus a local scene brief into an inspectable prose prompt, but
assistance and accepted prose do not silently become continuity authority.
Explicit non-goals are broad feature discovery, accessibility certification,
provider generation, a second segment, and filling optional fields for
coverage.

## Visible author journey

Create the project through the visible **Create** form using the receipt's
prepared parent path, folder, title, and a concise description derived from the
sealed intent. Verify the displayed project path and status.

Proceed in the order that feels natural from the UI:

1. In **Story Configuration**, express the story contract, close-third-past
   prose mode, mature treatment without diagnostic certainty, Clara's agency,
   and the prohibited content/plot outcomes.
2. In **Records**, author only the canonical material listed in the charter.
   Keep the family recollection, knowledge/reveal lock, physical geometry, and
   each character's current state explicit. Prefer atomic facts where the UI
   supports them, while allowing the visible rich-dossier shape for cast.
3. Use **Private Notes** only for a need already named in the scratchpad. Before
   leaving the surface, promote any adopted decision by re-authoring it into a
   canonical record; do not copy scratch into authority wholesale.
4. In **Active Working Set**, assign Tomás, Clara, Daniel, objects, and location
   records only to their intended visible function. Confirm Daniel is not
   onstage and that staff are pressure/setting, not accidental speakers.
5. In **Generation Brief**, fill the visible universal minimum and only useful
   conditional fields. For every populated prompt-facing field, first record
   its author need and intended observable effect. At minimum the saved brief
   must communicate: Tomás's POV and patient voice; room entry as the directive;
   the present physical/knowledge state; the bounded family evidence; Clara's
   precision and freedom to doubt; cast participation; the half-written letter
   and room/corridor/snow/woods geometry; all prohibited outcomes; and the exact
   reversible stop. Do not treat readiness/validation controls as prompt-facing
   unless the UI says they are.
6. Save and inspect readiness. Distinguish warnings from blockers and resolve
   blockers only through canonical visible controls. Never bypass validation.

After each consequential action, wait for the UI to settle and inspect fresh
visible output. At safe points, make a light check of focus visibility,
accessible names, help/label association, color-only state, clipping,
scrolling, long-form readability, and the 1440x900 layout. Capture a finding
before using accessibility-tree, geometry, or visible DOM-fragment diagnostics.

useful result. For the prose prompt and every assistance prompt invoked by the

Give each exact prompt to a different fresh no-parent-context executor. The
executor receives no premise, expected answer, suspected defect, prior output,

```text

Do not inspect the repository, any other file, the parent conversation, or prior responses.
Write the requested output verbatim to <absolute-response-file> instead of returning its contents.
```

If genuine fresh isolation is unavailable, stop with
`cold-subagent-unavailable`; do not simulate the response in the operator
context. Never inject an assistance response into provider-result UI.

Assess every raw response before editing or canonical adoption. For prose,
check local scope, directive, physical state, POV/knowledge, reveal locks, cast,
voice, content boundaries, unsupported durable change, genericity, quality, and
the exact stop. Record contract compliance, unsupported invention, omissions,
contradictions, adoption decision, repair burden, likely layer, verdict, and
bounded confidence. One unchanged-prompt retry in a different fresh context is
allowed only if the first prose response is materially poor or malformed. If
both attempts need replacement-level rewriting, stop with
`prose-quality-blocker`.

For each deliberately populated Generation Brief field, search the visible
inspector for its distinctive saved value and label. Record `present`, `absent`,
or `unclear`, placement/prominence, response evidence, and one of `used`,

## Independent challenges for important product claims

After direct observations and prompt assessments are sealed, select the two
most consequential product claims that the report would otherwise rely on,
regardless of whether each is a strength or problem. At least one should test a
claim tied to a critical gate—readiness/prompt fidelity or visible acceptance
and non-automatic continuity authority. Do not challenge a merely literary
preference as if it were a product fact.

For each claim, create a minimal privacy-safe challenge packet under `/tmp`
containing only the exact claim, the directly visible observation(s), and the
smallest selected screenshot or excerpt needed to assess it. Dispatch one fresh
no-parent-context challenger per claim. Tell the challenger to inspect only its
packet, separate observation from inference, identify alternative explanations,
and return `supported`, `narrowed`, `contradicted`, or `insufficient` with a
bounded confidence and rationale. It must not inspect the repository, product
source/docs, project files, other evidence, the report draft, or prior responses.

Do not let a challenge erase the original observation. Revise only the scope or
confidence justified by its result and use exactly one appropriate evidence tag
(`independent-supported`, `independent-narrowed`,
`independent-contradicted`, or `independent-insufficient`) alongside the direct
visible basis. Record the actual count in `independent_claim_challenges`
(planned count: 2), summarize the challenge and limitation in the relevant
finding, and delete raw challenge packets/responses after validation.

## Candidate, acceptance, and continuity gate

Only after raw prose judgment, click **Write or paste candidate**, paste the
preferred response from its temporary file through the visible flow, and make
only ordinary author edits. Record burden as `none`, `light`, `substantial`, or
`rewrite`. Assistance and prose remain non-authoritative throughout.

Accept once only when the candidate is coherent and all sealed bounds still
hold. Visit **Accepted Segments** and visibly verify a new positive sequence
(expected sequence `1` for this fresh project), its endpoint, and the accepted
state. Capture minimal privacy-safe proof that does not expose the accepted
prose. Inspect the durable-change reminder. Then manually update only the
canonical records or Generation Brief items the author chooses—for example,
Clara's reversible postponement, any change to the letter, or the exact next
unresolved response point. If Segment Reconciliation is sincerely invoked,

still offstage and no prohibited outcome became canon. Do not compile or accept

Completion is forbidden without visible sequence proof and explicit canonical
post-acceptance review. Accepted prose itself is never evidence that durable
continuity changed.
