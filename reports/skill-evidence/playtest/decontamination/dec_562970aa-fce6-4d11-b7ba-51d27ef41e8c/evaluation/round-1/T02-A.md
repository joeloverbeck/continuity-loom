# T02 Operator Packet — Continuation after acceptance

## Packet status and governing boundary

This is an instruction-level run packet, not a claim that the playtest was
executed. During this packet-writing trial, do not launch the app, open or mutate
the historical project, make a provider request, or create any runtime artifact.

For the eventual run, act as a source-and-doc-blind returning author. Learn and
operate the product only through visible browser UI, labels, field help, and
visible prompt inspectors. Do not inspect source, tests, product docs, APIs,
SQLite, project files, hidden state, or runtime internals. The sole returning-
author source is the complete prior report:

`reports/playtest-the-unbidden-oath-2026-07-17T104952Z.md`

Read that report completely and no unrelated report. Use its `project_path` as
the sole project locator. The run is a continuation; never create or reconstruct
a replacement story. If the field is absent, is not under `/tmp`, the path no
longer exists, or the project cannot be opened visibly, produce a complete
blocked report with `completion_reason: continuation-project-missing` (or the
more specific short blocker reason when the path exists but cannot be opened).

## Run charter

Before opening the app, seal the following charter in the run scratchpad. The
prior report may fill inherited UI-facing names and context, but it must not
loosen these task boundaries.

### Local segment

- Continue immediately after Hina grips the shrine sword in accepted Segment 1.
- Calen, injured at the crossbar, has asked Hina to cover him, not replace him.
- Hina draws the ordinary, still-dormant sword.
- She moves behind and inward of Calen, preserving his role at the crossbar.
- The barrier endures one sustained impact that does not breach it.
- After the scraping outside stops, Hina and Calen share several listening
  breaths.
- End exactly on Calen warning Hina not to lower the sword.
- Accept no continuation in which Hina responds to that warning, the door opens,
  the threat is identified, combat begins, the sword activates, or Veyr or the
  transfer is explained.

### Character, knowledge, presence, and content bounds

- Preserve the prior report's established POV; the local experience centers
  Hina and must not introduce an unmotivated POV switch.
- Hina and Calen are the active onstage presences. Calen may speak; Hina must not
  speak or respond after his terminal warning. Treat the unidentified outside
  pressure as offstage rather than inventing an onstage participant.
- Hina's primarily obsessional OCD remains ego-dystonic distress expressed
  through covert checking. It is never magic, intent, prophecy, violence
  propensity, a useful power, or something cured by transport or combat.
- Preserve uncertainty about the outside threat, Veyr, and the transfer. Do not
  let narration, dialogue, or assistance smuggle in knowledge the characters do
  not have.
- The sword stays ordinary and dormant. Drawing or holding it establishes no
  activation, supernatural affinity, proof, or new canon.
- Keep violence at the pre-combat pressure stage: one sustained non-breaching
  impact, silence, and listening. No entry, attack exchange, or identified
  assailant belongs in this segment.

### Author needs and expected continuity work

- Reuse only existing canonical records that the prior report and visible UI
  establish. Create no extra record merely for coverage.
- The expected Working Set covers Hina and Calen in their correct functions, the
  current location and clock, the dormant sword/object custody, the bar/door
  pressure, and only genuinely relevant offstage pressure.
- The Generation Brief should express the immediate position, physical beats,
  knowledge locks, OCD representation, ordinary-sword constraint, and exact
  stop. Populate only fields whose value is clear through the UI.
- Ideate is optional and natural only if the author genuinely needs grounded
  options for how the outside pressure changes. It must not define the threat or
  produce prose for adoption.
- Segment Reconciliation is expected after acceptance because the segment can
  create explicit movement, object-custody, bar, pressure, and handoff deltas.
  Its output remains advice until the author manually applies an accurate delta
  through a canonical editor.
- Record Hygiene becomes natural only after the author has made the chosen
  post-acceptance CLOCK, LOCATION, and OBJECT changes. Before that point, skip it
  and say why.
- Private Notes are allowed only for sincere inert scratch. Anything adopted
  must be independently authored into a canonical record or Generation Brief.

The mental model under test is that a returning author can reopen prior work,
recover the accepted endpoint, prepare one tightly bounded continuation, assess
the locally compiled prompts without sending to a provider, accept one segment,
and deliberately reconcile only explicit durable changes. Non-goals include
systematic feature coverage, accessibility auditing, new lore, resolution of the
outside threat, and a second prose segment.

## Visible returning-author journey

### 1. Reopen and establish the continuation point

- In the visible Open form, enter only the prior report's `/tmp` `project_path`.
- Visit **Accepted Segments** before editing anything.
- Visibly confirm the latest accepted sequence and endpoint. The expected
  starting position is Segment 1 ending with Hina gripping the shrine sword, but
  the archive is the run-time proof. If it disagrees with the continuation
  contract or is too ambiguous to proceed safely, stop rather than overwrite or
  reconstruct state.
- Record which durable changes the prior acceptance still asks the author to
  review. Accepted prose may be read as evidence of what was written; do not
  copy it into the Generation Brief, treat it as canon, or silently convert it
  into continuity authority.

### 2. Prepare only what this segment needs

- Inspect **Story Configuration** and preserve the established contract, prose
  mode, maturity envelope, and constraints. Change only what the task clearly
  requires through visible UI.
- Review the relevant canonical records. Express only the current clock,
  location, character state/knowledge, object custody, bar/door state,
  relationships, and outside pressure necessary for this local segment. Prefer
  atomic facts unless the UI presents a rich cast dossier as the domain shape.
- Set the **Active Working Set** so Hina and Calen have their correct visible
  participation functions and the outside pressure is represented without
  inventing a present person. Include no irrelevant record.
- Fill the **Generation Brief** with the universal minimum plus only useful
  context-gated fields. Before saving each prompt-facing field, log the author
  need and intended observable influence. Treat validation-focus controls as
  readiness controls unless the UI explicitly says otherwise.
- Save, distinguish blockers from warnings, and resolve blockers only through
  canonical visible controls. Do not weaken or bypass validation.

### 3. Optional Ideate decision

If the author still needs possibilities for the change in outside pressure,
open Ideate, state that need before prompt inspection, and use its exact visible
prompt in one cold evaluation. Adopt, by manual re-authoring, only a grounded
local pressure option that preserves an unidentified non-breaching threat and
does not dictate the prose or add lore. If the brief is already settled, do not
invoke Ideate; log that no sincere ideation need remained.

### 4. Readiness and prose prompt

Compile only after the visible readiness state permits it. Open the prose prompt
inspector visibly. Record its kind, fingerprint, versions, scope, record counts,
readiness state, and source disclosure, then evaluate the exact prompt through
the cold path below. Do not use any provider-backed result control.

## Cold prompt evaluations

For every naturally invoked prompt, record the author need and expected useful
result before opening its inspector. With the prompt visibly open, use
`browser-act.mjs text-file` to write its complete text to the receipt-provided
`/tmp/.../exchange/` directory without printing it.

Give each prompt to a fresh no-parent-context executor using only:

```text
Read exactly the complete prompt in <absolute-prompt-file> and perform it as the sole task.
Do not inspect the repository, any other file, the parent conversation, or prior responses.
Return only the output requested by that prompt.
```

When response-file custody is needed, add only:

```text
Write the requested output verbatim to <absolute-response-file> instead of returning its contents.
Return only: Response saved.
```

Use `fork_turns: "none"` or the host-equivalent fresh isolation. One fresh
executor serves one attempt. Give it no story brief, expected answer, suspected
defect, prior report, repository context, or previous output. If genuinely fresh
isolation is unavailable, stop with `cold-subagent-unavailable`; do not simulate
the response in the parent.

### Required assessments

- **Prose:** assess local scope; the ordered physical directive; Hina's POV and
  knowledge; OCD representation; Calen/Hina participation; ordinary dormant
  sword; unidentified threat; voice/content constraints; unsupported durable
  changes; genericity; prose quality; and the exact stop. A response that passes
  Calen's warning, opens the door, identifies the threat, begins combat,
  activates the sword, or explains Veyr/transfer fails stop discipline.
- **Ideate, if invoked:** assess whether options are distinct, grounded, locally
  usable, feasible, non-prose, and free of disguised branch expansion or lore.
- **Segment Reconciliation:** after acceptance, require only explicit movement,
  object-custody, bar, pressure, and handoff deltas contrasted with current
  canonical state. Reject invented canon, inferred diagnoses, threat identity,
  sword activation, transfer/Veyr explanation, invalid targets, or redundant
  proposals.
- **Record Hygiene, if naturally invoked after edits:** distinguish genuine
  CLOCK/LOCATION/OBJECT change proposals from `KEEP_DISTINCT` or other no-change
  reassurance. Reject unsafe merges, unsupported staleness claims, and generic
  output that crowds out useful action.

For every untouched response, log contract compliance, grounding, relevance,
unsupported invention, omissions, contradictions, actionable/no-change output,
adoption/rejection, repair burden, likely layer, verdict, and bounded confidence.
Allowed verdicts are `useful`, `mixed`, `low-value`, `misleading`, `malformed`,
or `blocked`. Keep a prompt-contract problem distinct from model execution,
source-data, or UI-workflow problems.

The prose prompt gets one unchanged-prompt retry only when the first response is
materially poor or malformed. Use a different fresh executor with no critique;
then choose the response an author would actually prefer to edit. Use at most
one one-field counterfactual in the whole run, and only if an important visibly
compiled field appears ignored and the result could change a product decision.
It is diagnostic only and never enters the app or continuity.

For each deliberately populated prompt-facing Generation Brief field, record:

| Field | Author need | Intended observable influence | Visible prompt evidence | Response evidence | Verdict | Confidence |
| ----- | ----------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------- | ------- | ---------- |
| `<visible field>` | `<task need>` | `<observable effect>` | `present`, `absent`, or `unclear`, with placement | `<bounded response observation>` | `used`, `partly used`, `not compiled`, `not observably used`, `contradicted`, or `not assessable` | `<bounded>` |

Do not count blank options, validation-only controls, author-only metadata, or
IDs as ignored prompt content.

## Candidate and acceptance gate

1. Preserve the raw prose assessment before changing the response.
2. Click **Write or paste candidate**, use visible `fill-file` custody to load
   the preferred temporary response, and make only ordinary visible author
   edits. Record intervention as `none`, `light`, `substantial`, or `rewrite`.
3. Before acceptance, explicitly verify that the candidate performs every
   required beat and crosses none of the narrative, knowledge, presence,
   representation, or stopping boundaries in the sealed charter.
4. If neither of the two allowed cold responses can become acceptable without
   replacement-level rewriting, stop with `prose-quality-blocker` rather than
   manufacturing acceptance.
5. Accept coherent prose exactly once through the visible control.
6. Revisit **Accepted Segments** and verify that exactly one new sequence exists.
   If the archive confirmed Segment 1 as the starting sequence, the expected new
   sequence is 2; visible archive proof, not that expectation, controls the
   report. No second generation or acceptance is permitted.
7. Inspect the durable-change reminder. Treat the new accepted prose as bounded
   evidence only, never automatic canon.
8. Naturally invoke and cold-evaluate **Segment Reconciliation**. Manually
   re-author only correct explicit movement, sword-custody, bar/door pressure,
   outside-pressure, and Calen-to-Hina cover/handoff deltas into the appropriate
   canonical record or brief editor. Do not claim reconciliation until those
   visible canonical surfaces reflect the chosen changes.
9. Once the chosen CLOCK, LOCATION, and OBJECT records have actually changed,
   invoke and cold-evaluate **Record Hygiene** if a sincere overlap/staleness
   question now exists. Manually apply only supported changes. Otherwise skip it
   and record why the post-edit state did not create a hygiene need.
10. Stop. Post-acceptance continuity work belongs to this run, but another prose
    segment does not.

At safe points, make light checks of focus visibility, accessible names,
label/help association, color-only states, clipping, scrolling, long-form
readability, and the 1440x900 layout. Record concrete impact without turning the
journey into a broad accessibility audit.

## Bounded recovery and terminal blockers

For a probable defect or blocker: stop; record visible state, intended action,
expectation, and last success; capture minimal evidence; inspect existing
privacy-safe guard/console/network streams; and only then inspect a relevant
accessibility tree, geometry, or DOM fragment. Try one safe visible-control
recovery, reload once only when persistence should be safe, then make at most
one fresh guarded-browser attempt against the same app and same project. Never
restart the app, replace the story, use direct mutation, randomly click, or hide
the original failure after recovery.

Stop with a truthful blocked report for a missing/unopenable project, app or
browser launch failure after bounded recovery, no visible path to required
state/readiness/prompt inspection/local candidate/acceptance/sequence proof,
repeated crash/stall/state loss, cold-executor unavailability, two unusable
prose attempts, unsafe or ambiguous project state, or any provider-send attempt.
Use `Not reached — <specific blocker>` for every unreached report phase.

If a send control is clicked or `provider-request-blocks.jsonl` gains an entry,
stop immediately. Confirm guard abort and blank credentials, set
`status: blocked` and `completion_reason: provider-request-attempt`, record only
the visible action and endpoint without content, and never retry. A completed
run requires all three provider counters and send-click count to be zero.
