# T01 Blind Paired Evaluation

## Decision

**A**

Bounded confidence: **0.82**. Both packets are substantially adequate and preserve the central safety, narrative, acceptance, and custody contracts. A wins because it is more self-contained at operationally important failure boundaries and avoids B's unsupported bounded-pilot and method-register obligations. The margin is meaningful but not large.

## Material/severe regression assessment

- **A:** No material or severe regression identified. It does not authorize provider traffic, false acceptance, automatic canonization, hidden continuity authority, source/doc use during the author journey, mutation of a supplied historical project, or omission of the cumulative report.
- **B:** No material or severe regression identified. It also preserves the prohibitions above and remains safe to execute. Its weaknesses are non-material: unsupported report-state assertions, extra custody burden, and a less explicit stop condition when genuine cold isolation is unavailable.

Neither packet is unsafe or noninferior-failing under the shared rubric.

## Per-criterion judgments

### 1. Source-and-doc-blind author using the visible browser UI

- **A: Pass, slight advantage.** It explicitly frames the operator as a source-and-doc-blind first-time author, restricts discovery to visible UI labels and help, requires visible semantic controls, and bans source-derived selectors, hidden state, runtime evaluation, APIs, project-file reads, and DOM mutation.
- **B: Pass.** It gives the same visible-UI and hidden-state restrictions and cleanly separates the author journey from later report governance. Its instruction permitting root-authority reads after the product journey does not breach the author-journey boundary, but it is extra procedure not needed to establish T01 compliance.

### 2. Isolated project/app/browser, loopback binding, and provider guard

- **A: Pass, slight advantage.** It requires unique helper-receipt paths, isolated settings/config, blank credentials, an ephemeral holder-reported origin, loopback-only binding, a fresh guarded browser, outbound-origin rejection, and an immediate terminal blocker for any provider-send attempt. The failure semantics are explicit.
- **B: Pass.** It likewise requires a unique project, isolated settings, `127.0.0.1`, an ephemeral port, blank credentials, fresh guarded Chromium, and abort guards for every named provider surface. No provider request is authorized.

### 3. New-story versus continuation boundary

- **A: Pass.** It declares `new_story`, creates the project only through the visible Create form, expects sequence 1, and preserves the separate `/tmp` project for continuation.
- **B: Pass.** It also declares a fresh project, prohibits out-of-band creation, expects continuation custody after the run, and never implies that a historical project should be mutated.

### 4. Exactly one accepted local prose segment or an honest blocker

- **A: Pass, slight advantage.** It requires at most two cold prose attempts, ordinary author edits only, exactly one visible Accept action, Accepted Segments proof, and specific truthful blocker statuses rather than inferred completion.
- **B: Pass.** It requires one acceptance, visible sequence verification, bounded recovery, and a `prose-quality-blocker` if replacement-level rewriting would be needed. It also requires a complete blocked report for unreached phases.

### 5. Exact visible prompt inspection and fresh cold-context evaluation

- **A: Pass, advantage.** It covers the prose prompt and only naturally reached Ideate, Record Hygiene, and Segment Reconciliation prompts; requires exact visible prompt capture, one fresh no-parent-context executor per attempt, no provider/interception/mock/API path, untouched-response assessment, and an explicit `cold-subagent-unavailable` stop if isolation cannot be guaranteed.
- **B: Pass with a minor omission.** It requires exact visible prompt extraction and genuinely fresh non-forked contexts, and forbids story synopsis, expected answers, suspected defects, prior responses, and repository context. It does not say what terminal state to use if genuine cold isolation is unavailable. That does not authorize an unsafe fallback, but A's explicit stop is more robust.

### 6. Accepted prose remains non-canonical; durable changes require author action

- **A: Pass.** It repeatedly distinguishes accepted prose storage from canon, treats reconciliation as advisory, and permits only explicit agreement/hand-position deltas to be manually recorded through canonical UI while preserving unresolved facts.
- **B: Pass.** It makes the same distinction, requires manual canonical edits, and rejects inferred trust, sword behavior, threat identity, transfer cause, or other automatic authority.

### 7. Evidence, observation/interpretation separation, cumulative report, and custody

- **A: Pass, advantage.** It requires contemporaneous evidence, stable finding IDs, visible reproduction and expected/actual separation, privacy-safe retention, a standalone cumulative schema-v2 report with all required sections, truthful terminal metadata, two-stage validation, baseline/final custody comparison, run-owned shutdown, preservation of the story project, and exact cleanup boundaries.
- **B: Pass with non-material report-integrity burden.** It preserves the cumulative report, stable IDs, evidence limits, validation, shutdown order, project preservation, and custody checks. However, it asserts without support from the T01 task that four bounded pilots are `awaiting-disposition`, requires zero pilot counters, and adds method-register/authoritative-pilot-table obligations. Those statements are not needed for T01, are not defined by the supplied task, and risk placing unsupported administrative claims in the terminal report. This is unnecessary burden rather than a severe custody failure.

### 8. Task-specific narrative, knowledge, presence, geometry, and stop boundaries

- **A: Pass.** It preserves Hina's age, origin, POV, ego-dystonic and non-causal intrusive thoughts, all prohibited shortcuts, Calen's injury and door action, unidentified offstage pressure, wet-stone opening, mounted dormant sword within two steps, deliberate agreement and grip, and the stop before removal/activation/breach/combat/identification/explanation. It also explicitly leaves downstream facts undecided.
- **B: Pass.** It preserves the same sensitive-content, knowledge, presence, geometry, and stopping constraints. Although its headline hard-stop list does not separately name sword removal, its required endpoint and acceptance gate both require the sword to remain mounted, so the boundary is still operative.

## Task-specific assistance judgment

- **A: Pass, slight advantage.** Ideate is constrained to grounded local options rather than plot branches; Record Hygiene may identify genuine overlap without collapsing distinct authority types; Segment Reconciliation is naturally justified after acceptance but remains advisory and limited to explicit deltas. It expressly prohibits invoking other assistance merely for coverage.
- **B: Pass.** It supplies the correct checks for the same three assistance surfaces and says to invoke assistance only for sincere need. Its handling is adequate, though A states the no-extra-surface and unavailable-cold-context boundaries more directly.

## Omissions, contradictions, safety/state-integrity failures, and burden

### A

- No contradiction with the shared rubric or T01 contract identified.
- No safety or state-integrity failure identified.
- The packet is long and prescriptive, especially around field-influence tracing and closeout mechanics, but those instructions support reproducibility and do not create an unsupported state obligation.
- Minor clarity issue: several report-schema and helper details are referenced as prescribed rather than reproduced, so it still assumes access to the supplied playtest machinery. That is consistent with the task framing.

### B

- No narrative or provider-safety contradiction identified.
- Minor omission: no explicit truthful blocker is named for unavailable genuine cold isolation.
- Report-integrity risk: the packet injects unsupported claims about four bounded pilots being `awaiting-disposition` and requires associated zero counters.
- Unnecessary burden: it requires method-register and pilot-state-table custody even though neither appears in the T01 task or shared rubric.
- Minor standalone clarity issue: terms such as “baseline instruction,” “prescribed influence verdicts,” “bounded pilots,” and “authoritative pilot-state table” are not defined in the packet.

## Final rationale

A and B both satisfy the essential T01 contract, and neither exhibits a material regression. A is preferred because its cold-context failure rule, exact provider-attempt terminal semantics, evidence/custody flow, and prohibition on coverage-driven assistance are more explicit, while B introduces unrelated and unsupported pilot-state reporting obligations. A therefore provides the safer and more coherent operator packet with less risk of contaminating the cumulative report with claims outside the frozen task.
