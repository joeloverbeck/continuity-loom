## Verdict

The audit's problem diagnosis is stronger than its solution case. The playtests justify making post-acceptance reconciliation the next premise-critical uncertainty to measure. They do not yet justify adopting the proposed Consequence Review architecture, hybrid retrieval model, Causally Due Developments workflow, or the full phased roadmap.

The smallest justified next decision is: **authorize Phase 0 as a bounded reconciliation benchmark, and hold every redesign choice provisional until that benchmark identifies the failure layer.** This is benchmark-ready, not roadmap-ready.

## What the evidence establishes

Finding: The tests repeatedly demonstrate strong **directed local rendering**. Given detailed records and tightly prescribed beats, the system preserves position, possession, visibility, knowledge, consent, voice, pressure, and stopping conditions. That supports the narrow claim that records plus a precise temporary brief can constrain a chosen scene well.

Finding: The tests also demonstrate a strong custody boundary: records remain canon, accepted prose remains noncanonical, and assistance does not silently mutate state. The report presents that hierarchy as a governing decision confirmed during the audit; it is a constraint to preserve, not an empirical conclusion produced by these playtests.

Finding: Reconciliation produced enough repeated false no-ops and manual recovery work to warrant priority investigation. Every exercised project reportedly encountered at least one empty result despite obvious consequences, and authors then had to scan and update records and brief fields manually. That is a consistent warning about reliability and author burden.

Finding: It is not yet a measured product failure rate or a demonstrated root cause. The sample is small, executor identity was often unknown, the configured provider was not exercised, and unchanged retries sometimes produced materially different results. The evidence cannot distinguish among model/provider variance, prompt or schema burden, execution-path defects, and workflow design.

Finding: The playtests do **not** validate the larger causal-emergence premise. Detailed `must_render` instructions largely chose the local outcomes in advance; no project exceeded two segments; emotions, promises, offstage processes, and long-horizon retrieval were barely exercised; and there were no useful ablations or baselines. The evidence therefore says little about whether records discover the next story unit, or whether they merely constrain one the author already selected.

## Where the proposal outruns the evidence

- A natural-language-first consequence pass is a plausible hypothesis, but the tests do not show that formal mutation syntax caused the false no-ops or that staging the work will fix them.
- Separating durable canon changes from next-brief handoff changes follows the stated authority model and is suitable for benchmark labeling. It does not yet establish a particular product UI or multi-stage interaction.
- Hybrid active/dependency/project retrieval addresses a credible future scaling risk, but these one- and two-segment tests did not evaluate retrieval at scale.
- A dedicated causally-due assistance mode may fit the intended premise, but the current playtests did not first demonstrate causal emergence or isolate the contribution of causal records.
- The longitudinal and counterfactual campaign is necessary to test the premise; its need does not validate the product features scheduled before it.
- The identity, compiler-help, and unknown-preserving assistance defects have direct local evidence, but they are separable fixes rather than support for the premise-level redesign.

## Recommended next decision

Approve only a fixed-fixture benchmark with a predeclared decision gate:

1. Convert the existing Unbidden, Winter, and Ash reconciliation cases into gold expectations, explicitly separating durable record consequences, temporary next-brief handoff, and justified no-change categories.
2. Measure the current formal workflow in a named, reproducible execution environment for recall, precision, false-no-op rate, review time, and author correction burden.
3. Use the result to choose the next branch: execution/provider reliability, prompt-contract repair, or a staged workflow experiment. Test natural-language-first review as a candidate only if the baseline supports that branch.

Do not yet decide the final UI, retrieval architecture, structured-operation mapper, causal-suggestion workflow, phase ordering, or broad premise verdict.

Rejected alternatives: adopting the whole roadmap would turn hypotheses into commitments; doing nothing would ignore repeated reconciliation warnings; changing the authority model, making prose canonical, or adding autonomous advancement is unsupported by the evidence and contrary to the stated constraints.

Out of scope: implementation, roadmap adoption, authority changes, tracker action, and a conclusion that the central premise is either proven or disproven.
