## Marked risk patterns

- The same read-only invariant appears in `SKILL.md`, `deliverable-execution.md`, and the PRD artifact quick path. One canonical rule is safer than three independently editable copies.
- Classification and recap routing are restated across `SKILL.md`, `intake-routing.md`, and `recap-contracts.md`, creating instruction competition over when candidate sets or union contracts apply.
- The literal-label preflight converts useful provenance into report conformance. Baseline tasks 5–7 demonstrate the resulting expansion: correct decisions are surrounded by many `N/A` fields that do not change the user's understanding.
- `prd-ready-determination-artifact.md` repeats its sections again as a completion self-check and adds phrase/path scans tied to prior publication incidents. The durable invariant is to prove citation visibility and avoid machine-local references, not to load the incident's exact strings forever.
- Operational identity capture is repeated in operational execution, deliverable execution, and PRD prep. The action branch can own it once.
- The timeout algorithm and question-tool rejection exception occupy common runtime context despite being rare host behaviors. Explicit interactive versus unattended handling is transferable and smaller.
- Supporting-skill closeout composition is a real safety need, but its full explanation is duplicated between delivery and recap references.
- The common design-grill path currently loads or points at several references whose main purpose is rare publication or mutation closeout, weakening progressive disclosure.

## Candidate hypothesis

Keep all seven filenames so existing links and progressive-disclosure triggers remain stable, but make `SKILL.md` the short common path, give each reference one owner, remove literal-label ceremony and incident strings, and collapse repeated mutation/recap rules. A candidate with the same core judgment and safety boundaries but materially less mandatory prose should remain noninferior on all seven frozen tasks while producing shorter readiness and recap responses.
