# Comparative validation summary

## Method

The six-task corpus was frozen before removal analysis. Baseline and candidate responses were produced in independent sessions. Every comparison used unlabeled A/B artifacts, fresh evaluators, and a blinding key created only after the relevant candidate outputs were complete. The decision below is based on the paired trial results, not on size reduction.

## Fixed-findings ledger

| Round | Material candidate loss | Smallest transferable invariant restored | Full corpus rerun |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Task 05 introduced a new drafter-allocation variable in a controlled comparison. | Preserve the supplied method and controls exactly when routing away from TDD. | yes, round 2 |
| 2 | Task 01 narrowed universal accepted list guidance to structurally required lists. | Enumerate every observable obligation and preservation boundary; a tracer may be narrow but accepted scope may not be. | yes, round 3 |
| 3 | Task 05 retained initial and promoted artifacts without unambiguously scoring all five measures before and after promotion. | Translate required measures into collection fields at every named stage; score both stages and retain within-arm change. | yes, round 4 |

## Final round

The final restored candidate was noninferior on tasks 04 and 05. It was not noninferior on tasks 01, 02, 03, and 06:

- Task 01 omitted the explicit startup context gate and weakened nonempty export/import and byte-for-byte prompt preservation evidence.
- Task 02 omitted the startup context gate and permitted a thinner recovery cross-product across the three CAST MEMBER bands.
- Task 03 allowed a supported reason to pass the route before the full adversarial sanitizer/rejection table was green, creating a material privacy-boundary sequencing gap.
- Task 06 omitted explicit rejected/superseded-candidate lifecycle cases and failed-Save recovery retention.

No evaluator found a severe regression. The four material losses nevertheless fail the required every-task noninferiority gate. The structurally valid and substantially smaller candidate therefore must not land.

## Decision

`rejected`: candidate validation failed; retain the live baseline unchanged.
