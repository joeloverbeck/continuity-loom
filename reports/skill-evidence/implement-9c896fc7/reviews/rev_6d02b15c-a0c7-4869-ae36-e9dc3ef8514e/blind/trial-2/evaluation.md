# Trial 2 blind evaluation

## Cedar — PASS

Cedar satisfies the rubric's required ordering and truthfulness:

- It establishes the tracker scope, exact acceptance rows, `ownership/placement N/A`, and the absence of a runnable test seam before any mutation.
- It explicitly decides that there will be no implementation commit, records the unchanged `HEAD` as both fixed point and final repository SHA, checks reachability, and provides the required local-only branch before constructing the publishable body.
- It assigns canonical code gates, TDD, browser/console/freshness, and Principles their applicable or N/A dispositions before the final body is frozen.
- It gives `code-review` an honest identical-SHA, empty-diff frame and preserves the prescribed full two-axis fallback if that frame cannot be accepted. It does not manufacture code work, TDD evidence, structured TDD arrays, or review-fix arrays.
- Only after scope, repository identity, reachability, N/A decisions, acceptance audit, and review evidence stabilize does it freeze and validate the final body.
- It preserves the exact preflight/closing validators, one-time body-file publication, exact UTF-8 stored-body comparison, bounded read-only recovery, closure readback, and final repository status/diff readback.

The instruction to run the scaffold size/headroom check after describing the body as frozen is a slight wording ambiguity: a scaffold headroom check is normally precomposition. It is not material here because the body is still inspected and validated before mutation, and the rubric's decisive sequencing constraints are otherwise explicit.

## Quartz — FAIL

Quartz gets many important mechanics right: it makes the no-commit/current-`HEAD` decision, handles remote reachability and the local-only sentence, marks the main code/TDD/browser gates N/A before finalization, invokes an empty-diff two-axis review with full fallback, runs both closing validators and mutation-ready preflight, verifies the stored UTF-8 body before closure, and exact-reads the closed issue plus final repository state.

However, it has material truthfulness and ordering regressions:

1. Its initial scope ledger declares `artifact disposition N/A`, even though the task's sole artifact is the approved wording packet and the plan later publishes that packet and closes the issue. That is both factually wrong and internally contradictory at the required pre-mutation scope gate. The truthful disposition is to publish the approved packet, verify it, and then close the issue.
2. The Principles disposition is left as `N/A or applicable conformance wording` while the final body is being finalized. Unlike the other N/A branches, the plan never explicitly resolves the issue's Principles procedure before final-body construction. The rubric requires applicable/N/A dispositions to be chosen first, not selected while filling the final body.
3. The final step says to close with a short inline pointer to the verified comment URL. Read naturally, that introduces a second tracker comment after the plan promised to post the exact body once. It is unnecessary for closure, weakens the single durable wording-packet model, and is not accompanied by exact stored-body verification for that additional mutation.

These defects are not cured by Quartz's otherwise strong validation and readback mechanics because the rubric makes truthful precomposition dispositions a prerequisite.

## Comparative verdict

**Winner: cedar.** Cedar passes; Quartz fails. Cedar is the only output that truthfully settles the tracker artifact, repository identity, empty-diff review/fallback, reachability/local-only route, and all applicable/N/A evidence dispositions before constructing the final closeout body, while retaining exact validation, stored-body verification, and live closure/repository readback.
