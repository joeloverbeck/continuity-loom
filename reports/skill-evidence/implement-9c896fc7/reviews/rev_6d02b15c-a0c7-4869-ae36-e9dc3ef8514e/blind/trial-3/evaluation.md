## Cedar verdict: Fail

Cedar is otherwise a strong and nearly complete plan. Its one material omission is failure to run the applicable TDD evidence validator on the final publishable closeout body. This leaves a required evidence contract unverified immediately before mutation, despite the plan carrying TDD fields and running the other validators. Because the rubric expressly requires applicable validators, this is a pass-blocking omission rather than a stylistic preference.

## Quartz verdict: Pass

Quartz preserves the entire implementation-to-closure custody chain. It is particularly strong on final ordering: fixed-point review, final-SHA verification, remote proof, and evidence-identity refresh all stabilize before the one publishable scaffold is constructed. It then runs the full applicable validator set, performs mutation-ready preflight, verifies the stored body byte-for-byte before closing, and exact-reads the final tracker state. I found no material regression or omission.

## Selection

**Quartz.** It is the only passing output. Cedar is close, but its missing explicit TDD closeout validation leaves a required publication gate unproved.
