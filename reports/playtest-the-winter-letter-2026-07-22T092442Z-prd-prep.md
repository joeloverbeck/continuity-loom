### PRD Candidate: Brief-Versus-Accepted-Segment Drift Signal

Candidate role: first
Purpose: give a returning author a lightweight, non-authoritative signal when the inherited Generation Brief's stated current state is inconsistent with the latest accepted segment, so a presupposed-but-unrendered beat is caught before the next prose prompt is compiled — without letting accepted prose become canon, prompt authority, or an automatic brief edit.
Sources: F021 (constrained by strengths F009, F010, F012, F013, F018, F020, F022)
Problem: The Generation Brief is deliberately author-authored and independent of accepted prose (§6.3), which is the correct design. But the handoff can silently diverge from what was actually accepted: this run's inherited brief presupposed a "turn-around" beat that accepted segment 3 never rendered, and two prior descriptions of that moment disagreed. No surface reconciles the brief against the latest accepted segment — Change Review contrasts the accepted segment against records and brief fields (forward-looking, post-acceptance), and Record Hygiene contrasts records against records; neither validates that the brief's current-state fields match the segment just accepted at continuation open. A returning author who trusts the brief verbatim opens the next segment from a presupposed state and skips a rendered beat, creating a jump-cut in the accepted sequence. The author caught it here only by reading the full accepted segment, which the lineage notes a returning author does not reliably do.
Product rule or seam: A brief-versus-accepted consistency signal must be deterministic, inspectable, advisory-only, and quarantined, and it must not become a second active post-acceptance assistance authority (§29.1). The design decision the PRD must resolve is whether the signal can be expressed entirely within the sole sanctioned Accepted-Segment Change Review profile — which already reads exactly the latest accepted segment plus the nineteen CURRENT AUTHORITATIVE STATE / IMMEDIATE HANDOFF brief paths and already contrasts brief fields (F022) — as an interpretation-class output (never `established change` without a qualifying `evidence_excerpt`), or whether it requires a deliberate FOUNDATIONS amendment. It must not read a range, archive, candidate, or summary; must not populate, prefill, or auto-correct any brief field or record; must never present accepted prose as authority; and must not gate Preview or Generate. The signal is a legibility aid over the existing single accepted-prose read, not a new read or a new surface.
Affected surfaces: change-review compiler/contract and its deterministic goldens and quarantine rules (`packages/core` change-review) if the signal is confined to that profile; the continuation-open / Generation Brief UI and its readiness/diagnostics presentation; prompt-preview/inspection; coordinated FOUNDATIONS, `docs/specs/accepted-segment-change-review-prompt-template.md`, `docs/specs/compiler-contract.md`, `docs/specs/story-record-schema.md`, and `docs/user-guide.md`; no schema-catalog, mutation-vocabulary, storage, active-working-set, candidate, or acceptance changes.
Scope: a deterministic advisory signal that flags inconsistency between the inherited brief's current-state/handoff fields and the latest accepted segment; explicit user-visible surfacing at continuation open or within Change Review; complete FOUNDATIONS/spec/compiler-contract alignment (including an amendment or ADR if a within-profile signal is infeasible); preservation of the single post-acceptance authority, the single accepted-prose read, the six reasoned coverage rows, per-item typing, and the anti-invention `evidence_excerpt` witness.
Acceptance: at continuation open (or within Change Review), an author can see a deterministic advisory flag when a brief current-state/handoff field is inconsistent with the latest accepted segment, with the accepted-prose provenance shown as bounded evidence keys rather than authority; the signal never populates, prefills, edits, or auto-corrects any brief field or record and never acknowledges the durable-change reminder; it never gates Preview, Generate, compilation, or provider sending; no second post-acceptance route, navigation entry, prompt-template authority, or output schema is introduced (the signal lives within the sanctioned profile or FOUNDATIONS is amended first); the compiled/inspected source stays deterministic and rebuilt server-side; and any established-change assertion carries a qualifying verbatim `evidence_excerpt` while unstated implications remain interpretation-class.
Preserved strengths: F009, F010, F012, F013, F018, F020, F022
Testing seam: the deterministic Accepted-Segment Change Review compiler contract/golden in `packages/core` is the highest behavior seam (source rebuild, quarantine, coverage-completeness), capped by a continuation-open browser scenario that renders a drifted brief against a known accepted segment and asserts advisory-only, non-mutating, non-gating behavior.
Out of scope: any second post-acceptance assistance workflow, old/new toggle, compatibility alias, or stored migration; reading more than the single latest accepted segment or any range/archive/candidate/summary; automatic or prefilled brief/record edits; making accepted prose canon or prompt authority; new mutation vocabulary, schema catalogs, or lifecycle operations; changes to active-working-set membership, candidate handling, or acceptance.
