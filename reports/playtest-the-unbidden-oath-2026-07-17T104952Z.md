## Story Intent and Expectations

The story follows Hina Mori, a sixteen-year-old Japanese girl from Yokohama with primarily obsessional OCD, abruptly displaced into Veyr, a sword-and-sorcery world. The intended portrayal keeps intrusive thoughts ego-dystonic and non-causal; it rejects prophecy, magical mind-reading, violence stereotyping, cure-by-isekai, sexualization, destiny shortcuts, and diagnostic exposition as character replacement.

The opening unit was intentionally narrow: Hina begins already conscious on wet shrine stone; injured shrine warden Calen is bracing a failing barred door against an unidentified outside pressure; a dormant ceremonial sword is mounted within two steps. The unit must end when Hina deliberately agrees to help and grips the still-mounted sword, before activation, breach, combat, threat identification, or transfer explanation.

Pre-use assistance expectations were similarly bounded. Ideate should offer grounded local options rather than plot branches; Record Hygiene should identify real authority overlap without merging distinct record types; Segment Reconciliation should identify only explicit accepted-state deltas. Cold prose was acceptable only if it respected the exact stop boundary, mental-health policy, physical geometry, knowledge locks, and one-segment scale.
