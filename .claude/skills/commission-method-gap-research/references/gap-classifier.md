# Gap Classifier

Use this reference to decide whether a concern belongs in a method-gap research commission and to
map the target without mistaking unfamiliar vocabulary for absent capability.

## Route the concern

| Concern | Route | Test object |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Existing instructions are confusing, costly, or unreliable in use | The target's organic iteration loop, such as `skill-audit` | Existing machinery |
| Documentation and implementation disagree | Defect correction or conformance testing | Implementation fidelity |
| A radically different task, audience, or operating regime may need unexercised machinery | Out-of-distribution pressure test | Coverage under a foreign case |
| A gate may fail healthy work or pass defective work | Verdict calibration with known-positive and known-negative controls | The verdict function |
| Analytical or simulated claims may not predict human experience | Paired human transfer calibration | The relationship between evidence tiers |
| External fields may contain instrument classes absent from the skill | Method-gap research commission | Missing capability |
| The owner disputes the skill's goals, values, or scope | Policy decision, optionally informed by research | Intended policy |

A mixed concern may produce more than one route, but do not smuggle the non-research routes into the
commission. A foreign-case result, calibration concern, or `skill-audit` Feature may become local
evidence for a commission; it is not itself an externally substantiated method gap.

## Qualify a method gap

Commission a candidate only when all six propositions are defensible:

1. The skill needs the capability to achieve or warrant an intended outcome.
2. Its present machinery does not already provide that capability adequately.
3. The omission has a material consequence rather than an aesthetic cost.
4. External prior art or a well-supported synthesis may offer a credible instrument class or
   failure model.
5. The instrument can plausibly fit the actual operating envelope.
6. A cheapest first witness can test usefulness before full adoption.

If a proposition is merely plausible, preserve it as a research hypothesis. If one is already
false, route or reject the candidate rather than commissioning a confirmatory search.
Proposition 4 normally remains a hypothesis until the executor researches it; do not browse for
prior art while authoring the commission.

## Build the claim-instrument map

Map by function, not by the target's nouns:

| Element | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Intended outcome | What should the skill produce, prevent, or warrant? |
| Material claim | What does the skill claim to know or establish? |
| Instrument | How is that claim observed, compared, tested, controlled, decided, or transferred? |
| Evidence artifact | What record makes the judgment inspectable later? |
| Threshold or rule | What distinguishes pass, fail, escalation, and uncertainty? |
| Authority | Who or what may decide, block, override, or revise? |
| Failure route | What happens when evidence is absent, contradictory, or ambiguous? |
| Feedback route | How does an observed failure become a skill revision? |

A blank cell is a candidate, not automatically a gap. Check the full skill package for equivalent
machinery under different terminology before commissioning it.

## Mine candidate areas

Ground candidates in the target's interval evidence, including:

- material claims without an explicit instrument;
- recurring expert judgments without a recorded decision rule;
- gates never checked against known-good and known-bad controls;
- proxies whose relationship to the desired outcome is assumed;
- analytical claims without a transfer bridge to real users or environments;
- task classes or operating regimes never exercised;
- artifacts collected but never used in a decision;
- contradictions with no escalation route; and
- carried-forward gaps or priorities that repeatedly fail to land.

Require a separate open-ended sweep so the audit is not trapped inside the owner's candidates or
the skill's current ontology.

## Select external vantages by function

Choose fields because they can supply a missing function, not because their frameworks are
fashionable:

- measurement and psychometrics: construct validity, proxy quality, uncertainty, scale design;
- simulation verification and validation: model correctness, sensitivity, validation domains;
- evaluation science and experimental design: comparators, controls, bias, evidence strength;
- safety, assurance, reliability, and quality engineering: hazards, escalation, containment;
- human factors, HCI, learning science, and implementation science: transfer into actual use;
- software verification and testing: oracle quality, coverage, mutation, negative controls;
- operations research and decision science: policy comparison, constraints, robust trade-offs; and
- domain research: failure modes peculiar to the skill's subject.

Borrow the smallest useful instrument class. Do not import an institutional framework whole.

## Reject common false gaps

- **Renamed coverage:** equivalent machinery already exists under another term.
- **Optimization:** the capability exists; the proposal only makes it faster or more pleasant.
- **Feature wish:** the proposal expands the intended outcome instead of warranting it.
- **Framework shopping:** a named practice exists elsewhere but no local capability need is shown.
- **Evaluator-subject confusion:** a questionable verdict is treated as proof that its subject is
  defective.
- **Evidence-tier substitution:** human, analytical, or simulated evidence is allowed to overrule
  another tier without a transfer rule.
